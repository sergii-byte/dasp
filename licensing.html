Why El Salvador

Understanding the El Salvador DASP License

El Salvador was the first country to make Bitcoin legal tender (Bitcoin Law D-57/2021), then the first to adopt a dedicated digital-asset statute (LEAD, 2023). The Bitcoin Law was reformed by D-199/2025 (in force from 30 April 2025) — Bitcoin remains legal tender with unlimited liberatory power, but acceptance is now voluntary and limited to natural persons + fully private legal entities; the State no longer accepts Bitcoin for taxes (Art. 4 derogated) and the Chivo state-wallet programme was wound down (Arts. 8–9 derogated). The DASP / LEAD framework, the BSP framework (D-27/2021 Reglamento) and the AML obligations are unchanged. The regime remains designed for serious operators — USD-denominated, low-tax, AML-aligned to FATF but lighter than the EU's MiCA, and explicitly open to stablecoins and foreign compliance staff.

01

Zero-tax regime

0% income, 0% capital gains, 0% VAT, 0% municipal on DASP-scope activity — effective from day 1 of registration LEAD Art. 36.

02

Dollarized economy

USD is legal tender since 2001. No currency conversion, no exchange-control risk, no FX mismatch with client operations.

03

Direct USDT on/off-ramp

Stablecoin exchange to and from USD is explicitly legal under LEAD. No third-country routing, no correspondent-banking workaround.

04

Lighter AML than MiCA

Calibrated to local compliance practice; senior-management approval only at EDD; no licensing capital requirements. FATF-aligned but pragmatic. Full operational thresholds in the AML Compliance guide.

05

8–12 months to licence

Five clearly-defined CNAD phases with statutory clocks (20 bd + 10 bd per cycle). Compare to 18–24 months under MiCA or FINMA process.

06

No residency for compliance roles

CO and Alternate CO may be foreign-resident — explicit DASP exemption Instr. Art. 84-B(2) Reform 1. Shareholders are not subject to residency requirements; the Representante Legal must be domiciled in El Salvador (typically a local lawyer under PoA).

07

Stablecoin-ready framework

CNAD has a dedicated Stablecoin Regulation with reserve rules and issuer registry — first fully-articulated stablecoin regime in the Americas.

08

BSP + DASP dual rail

Bitcoin operations under the Bitcoin Law D-57/2021 (BSP, BCR Registry); everything else under LEAD D-643/2023 (DASP, CNAD). One entity, two parallel licences. Reform D-199/2025 affects only retail Bitcoin acceptance — BSP framework intact.

Registration window — D-27/2021 Art. 3. Before carrying out operations, BSPs must present to BCR (within 20 business days after entry into force of the Regulation) the registration form + digital copy of the deed of incorporation + IDs of partners (or DUI / passport for natural persons). On discontinuation: BCR is informed immediately, and removal from the BSP Registry happens within 72 hours.

Supervision split — D-27/2021 Art. 5. BCR maintains the Registry; the Superintendency of the Financial System (SSF) has all supervision and regulation powers (per its Creation Law) over BSPs and may impose sanctions for breach of Art. 3 (registration) or Art. 4 (standards of conduct). Banks providing services to BSPs must implement appropriate controls.

Legal Framework

Applicable Legislation

Five instruments govern DASPs in El Salvador. The binding DASP stack is LEAD + RPSAD + LCLDA + Instructivo UIF + LECAT. The Bitcoin Law (D-57/2021 as reformed by D-199/2025) governs the parallel BSP track — included for completeness if the entity also handles Bitcoin. Outdated references to the pre-reform CNAD AML Guide have been removed across the portal.

LEAD — Ley de Emisión de Activos Digitales

Decree No. 643 of 9 January 2023. Primary statute for digital asset service providers, issuers and certifiers. Establishes CNAD authority, registration requirements, tax benefits and the sanctions framework.

Primary Law

RPSAD — Reglamento para Proveedores de Servicios de Activos Digitales

CNAD-035-2023. Detailed regulation for DASP registration, ongoing obligations, cybersecurity, customer service and operational rules. 48 articles.

DASP Regulation

LCLDA (Decree 426/2025) — AML Law

Decree No. 426 — Special Law for the Prevention, Control and Punishment of Asset Laundering, Financing of Terrorism, and Financing of the Proliferation of Weapons of Mass Destruction. Defines reporting entities (incl. DASPs via supervision by CNAD), CDD trigger (Art. 15), 24-hour STR (Art. 24), regulated transactions reporting (Art. 25), 15-year retention (Art. 26), tipping-off prohibition (Art. 44).

AML/CFT

Instructivo UIF (Reform 1 + Reform 2)

Operational rulebook from the Financial Investigation Unit. CDD measures (Art. 12), EDD (Art. 13), SDD (Art. 14), PEP (Arts. 16–18), beneficial ownership 10% floor (Art. 20) + UBO majority/control (Art. 21-A), Operación Regulada thresholds (Art. 51), Travel Rule data (Art. 52 par. 2 reformed), VASP-specific obligations (Art. 84-A registration, Art. 84-B general obligations, Art. 84-C reports).

UIF Guidelines

UIF Acuerdo 734/2022 — PSAV Guide

Guía PSAD-LDA-FT-ADM. UIF supervisory operational guide for Digital Asset Service Providers. Imperative DDC cascade (≤ $1K SDD / $1K–$4K Standard / > $4K EDD per § V.2.b.II — supersedes general FI thresholds), monthly client report, individual transaction report ≥ $1,000 within 5 wd, zero-transaction notification, eight transaction-type reporting forms (Anexo IV).

UIF · PSAV Operational

LECAT — D-108

Special Law against Acts of Terrorism. UNSC sanctions freeze obligation (Art. 37), terrorist designation before UNSC (Art. 34-B). Note: tipping-off prohibition is in LCLDA Art. 44, not in LECAT.

CFT

Issuer & Stablecoin Regulations

CNAD-017-2023 (Issuer Regulation) and Stablecoin Regulation. Govern public and private issuances, RID requirements, certifier process and stablecoin reserve rules. Separate registration track from DASP — included for completeness.

Issuance Framework

Bitcoin Law — D-57/2021 (reformed by D-199/2025)

Bitcoin as legal tender; D-199/2025 made acceptance voluntary (private actors only) and unwound the State-side regime (no BTC for taxes, Chivo wound down). Capital-gains exemption maintained. BSP track (BCR Registry, SSF supervision) lives in Reglamento D-27/2021 — unaffected by the Reform.

Asset Perimeter

Permitted & Restricted Digital Assets

Not every digital asset is marketable under a DASP licence. The applicant lists the digital assets it intends to sell or market — including benefits, restrictions, limits and any financial / commercial restrictions — at the registration stage LEAD Art. 20(b). CNAD then supervises the perimeter through its Art. 9(k)(l) authority, and any addition to the asset list requires a material change notification.

Permitted by default

  • Major transparent-ledger assets (BTC, ETH, SOL, TRX and similar)
  • Regulated stablecoins expressly authorised by CNAD (e.g. USDT, USDC)
  • Assets declared in the DASP registration file and accepted by CNAD

Prohibited or restricted

  • Privacy coins — prohibited in practice. Assets with limited or non-existent traceability (Monero XMR, ZCash ZEC and similar) are not approvable — CNAD operational position based on AML/CTF risk under LCLDA + traceability requirements CNAD operational practice
  • Algorithmic stablecoins — excluded from the stablecoin perimeter under the CNAD Stablecoin Regulation
  • Additional stablecoins — require express prior CNAD authorisation per the Stablecoin Regulation (covered by quarterly RPSAD Art. 16 reporting)
  • Tokens from issuances — separate CNAD analysis under the Issuer Regulation (CNAD-017-2023)

How restrictions are imposed. The applicant submits the list of intended digital assets at registration LEAD Art. 20(b). The list is locked at registration; any addition requires a material change notification to CNAD RPSAD Art. 9. Operating outside the authorised perimeter is a sanctionable breach under LEAD Arts. 38–40.

Cross-border reach. The pre-registration form requires the applicant to declare its intended operating area LEAD Art. 18. Serving clients outside the declared perimeter — or marketing to jurisdictions where digital-asset services are prohibited — is a compliance breach. DASPs must maintain a list of restricted jurisdictions and apply geofencing and KYC filters accordingly Instr. Art. 12(f) (high-risk jurisdictions).

Official CNAD procedure per the Guía de requisitos para el proceso de registro como Proveedores de Activos Digitales: two consecutive stages — Pre-inscription (Phase 1 + Phase 2) and Inscription (final stage). Per round: CNAD has 20 business days to issue resolution or observations RPSAD Art. 8; if observations, applicant has 10 bd to cure (+ up to 5 bd extension LPA Art. 97). Failure to cure triggers archive of the file LPA Art. 72.

Banking — operational note (not a regulatory requirement). Salvadoran retail banks do not, in practice, service international digital-asset flows. Many DASPs open a local account purely for operating expenses; client-fund custody, fiat on/off-ramps and settlement are typically routed through foreign correspondent banks, EMIs or stablecoin rails. A realistic banking strategy is normally part of the business plan reviewed by CNAD, but local-banking is an option, not a licence requirement practice.

No "registered agent" concept for S.A. de C.V. There is no US-style registered-agent service. Foreign branches must appoint a Salvadoran-resident legal representative via notarised power of attorney LEAD Art. 18(d).

Operational Standards

Conduct & Disclosure Obligations

RPSAD Arts. 17–37 set the conduct, ethics, disclosure, market-integrity and operational obligations that bind every DASP from go-live. Most are reviewed by CNAD during ongoing supervision and breach can trigger sanction (LEAD Art. 38) or precautionary suspension (RPSAD Art. 40).

Standards of conduct & good faith

Art. 17 imposes an impartiality obligation: DASPs may not put their own interests before clients'. Five concrete duties on top:

Investor priority rule RPSAD Art. 17(c)

For purchase / sale managed on own account and on behalf of clients: respect the principle of investor priority — first in time, first in law. No particular investor may be harmed.

No commission abuse RPSAD Art. 17(d)

Clients may not be induced to operations for the sole purpose of generating commissions. Refrain from operations whose only purpose is to receive or multiply commissions abusively without client benefit.

Price-formation transparency RPSAD Art. 17(e)

Contribute to transparency in market-price formation. Avoid public disclosure of false, inaccurate or biased information about digital assets.

Cooperation with CNAD RPSAD Art. 17(f)

Collaborate with CNAD's control and surveillance functions. Provide requested information / access on lawful demand.

Investment-recommendation discipline RPSAD Art. 17(g)

If DASP advised an investment and market variation / new info / risk changes affect the recommendation to the investor's detriment → notify the investor and request instructions. Statutory presumption: DASP acts in best interest at best possible price.

Sanction tier LEAD Art. 38(a)

Breach of CNAD-set good-conduct / ethics standards → fine up to 114 minimum salaries (≈ $46,603) per infraction. Repeat offences may escalate to Art. 38(b) tier (≈ $47K–$124,684).

Code of Ethics — Art. 18

Mandatory deliverable within 6 calendar months of final registration. Each DASP must prepare a Código de Ética and make it available to all clients on its website (URL) (or attach to offers). CNAD may require modifications. Code must be supervised, implemented and the supervision aspects must be communicated to CNAD. Ethics-Committee complaints + decisions visible to CNAD.

(a) General & specific principles

Principles governing the conduct of all employees.

(b) Ethical standards

Standards governing the behaviour of employees.

(c) Conflict-of-interest policy

Operations, investors, employees, directors, representatives.

(d) Fair treatment of clients

Documented policies on fair treatment.

(e) AML / CFT / CFP policies

Reference to the broader AML programme (Art. 19) integrated in the Code.

(f) Non-pecuniary penalty regime

For employee breaches of the Code. Penalties may not be financial in form.

Computer systems & operational platforms — Art. 20

If the DASP operates digital platforms / wallets / similar applications, the computer systems must perform at minimum the following four functions:

(a) Settlement quality

Quick, correct and accurate settlement of all transactions made on the platform / wallet.

(b) Electronic Book

Accurate, auditable and verifiable record of each transaction. Must include: date · type of transaction · participating users · total amount.

(c) Permanent monitoring

Real-time / near-real-time monitoring of all operations to detect errors and failures of any nature. Capability to recover damaged or deleted information from impacted operations or significant controversies.

(d) Account suspension/closure

Mechanism to suspend and close customer accounts that have breached the platform's terms and conditions.

Website (URL) disclosure — Art. 23

All communication related to the digital assets the DASP markets, promotes or manages must be available at all times at the responsible provider's website (URL — Spanish: LRU, Localizador de Recursos Uniformes). Display rules:

(a) Clear & accessible

Clear, transparent and easily accessible — no hiding behind login walls or buried clauses.

(b) Not misleading

Information presented must not be misleading or biased.

(c) Third-party citation

If information from a third party is cited, the source must be identified.

Required URL contents (illustrative, per Art. 23 + adjacent operational standards). Terms of use · contact info · SSF complaint channel notice · per-asset general explanation (interchangeability, current price, 12-month price history, current platform volume) · custody / loan / deposit complementary products with regular updates · Code of Ethics (after Art. 18 6-month deadline) · service-specific T&Cs · fee schedule · publicly disclosable wallet list. Update cadence: regular and reasonable per product. RPSAD Arts. 23, 18, 84-B(7) Reform 1

Personal-data protection — Art. 36

VASP is directly responsible for safeguarding acquirer / client / user personal information at all times. Confidentiality is mandatory.

  • Liability on leak: the DASP that suffered the leak must pay financial compensation to its clients AND pay the corresponding administrative fine.
  • This is a strict-liability regime — no fault requirement; the leak itself triggers the obligation.
  • Operationalised through cyber controls (RPSAD Art. 27) + AML record-keeping (LCLDA Art. 26 — 15 yr retention) + Code of Ethics fair-treatment policy (Art. 18(d)).
  • Implication for AML manual: leak-incident response procedure must include the (i) financial-compensation calculation method and (ii) CNAD notification track.

Privileged-information regime — Arts. 24–26 (insider / market integrity)

(Art. 24) Prohibition

No DASP may carry out operations with privileged information of issuers. Refrain from:

  • (a) Acquiring / transferring digital assets using privileged info — direct or indirect, own or third-party account
  • (b) Recommending acquisition / disposal based on privileged info, or inducing such action
  • (c) Recommending cancellation / modification of orders based on privileged info, or inducing such

(Art. 25) Accidental disclosure

If accidental access to issuer's privileged information occurs, the exposed executive / employee must disclose to the Compliance Officer (or designated conflict-of-interest manager).

Once disclosed: the DASP may continue operations with the affected issuer, provided the exposed individual is prohibited from any operation with that issuer and from sharing the info with any other employee.

(Art. 26) Communication restrictions

No DASP holding privileged information may communicate it to another natural or legal person, except when communication occurs in the normal exercise of work, profession or functions.

Document the communication channel and recipient in the case file.

LEAD Art. 21(k) market-integrity hook. DASPs must have and apply effective mechanisms to prevent price manipulation and maintain market integrity, established in Articles 3, 43 and 35 of LEAD. The privileged-information regime above is the operational baseline; CNAD may also resolve specific market-manipulation practices via reasoned resolution.

Investment refund obligation — Art. 37

For investments made through a DASP — 48-hour refund window on force majeure.

  • DASP guarantees the investment within the contracted term and conditions
  • In case of fortuitous event or force majeure: DASP must terminate the operation AND credit the client's wallet within forty-eight (48) hours with the funds delivered for the investment
  • Operational hook: the wallet-credit timing is independent of CNAD review or external dispute resolution — internal SLA must enable 48 h restoration
  • Document the force-majeure / fortuitous-event determination and the wallet-credit confirmation in the client file

CNAD precautionary suspension — Art. 40

Trade activities can be temporarily suspended by CNAD.

  • Trigger: suspicion or complaint of breach of DASP obligations
  • Maximum duration: 20 business days
  • Procedure: CNAD notifies the provider of investigation; if sufficient evidence collected → suspension proceeds → exhaustive inspection during the 20 bd window
  • Outcomes within suspension period:
    • Breach confirmed → corresponding sanction (LEAD Art. 38) + improvement plan ordered
    • No breach → CNAD annuls the provisional measure → DASP exercises all rights immediately
  • Practical implication: have an internal "trade-suspension response plan" parallel to the Resolution Plan — CNAD legal contact, client communication template (without tipping-off), revenue-impact assessment, regulator engagement protocol

External cybersecurity auditor. 3 mandatory scenarios verbatim from Art. 28.

RPSAD Art. 28 — three mandatory cybersecurity test scenarios (verbatim).

  • Scenario 1 — Confidentiality breach (Ruptura de Confidencialidad): theft or attempted theft of customer information and relevant operations.
  • Scenario 2 — Availability outage (Ruptura de la Disponibilidad): infrastructure services unavailable or communication between users / employees and infrastructure achieved only in a very limited form.
  • Scenario 3 — Integrity breach (Ruptura de la Integridad): critical infrastructure services under attack and integrity of stored information very difficult to guarantee.

Each scenario test must include: (i) vulnerability assessment with remediation; (ii) scenario-based exercises evaluating response/resolution/recovery plans, contemplating extreme but possible scenarios with realistic attack models; (iii) invasion / intrusion testing. Reaction and contingency plans required for each scenario.

  • 115 to 305 minimum salaries (≈ $47K–$124,684) per infraction LEAD Art. 38(b): trading unauthorised digital assets; refusing to comply with CNAD resolutions; refusing to provide CNAD-requested information; providing wrong information without correction; market-manipulation actions.
  • 20% surcharge on unpaid fine 5 working days after firm status LEAD Art. 38(b)(6).

Exit

Cessation & Wind-down

RPSAD Arts. 38–39 contemplate two types of cessation. Both follow the same request procedure with CNAD; differences are in scope, timing and downstream obligations. The wind-down plan must protect client assets — return digital assets and fiduciary money to owners.

Temporary Cessation

Pause & resume
  • Action plan with cessation date + planned resumption date
  • Procedures to protect digital assets + fiat held under custody
  • Procedures to refund assets + fiat to owners on request
  • Maximum suspension window: 20 business days from declared cessation date
  • Mandatory resumption of operations within that window — failure → automatic conversion to definitive cessation grounds
For cyber outages / system migration / liquidity stress / ownership change. RPSAD Art. 39(b)

Definitive Cessation

Permanent exit
  • Date of cessation of activities
  • Liquidation plan for assets and employees
  • Procedure to return digital assets + fiduciary money to owners
  • CNAD grants minimum 20 business days (typically more, per applicant request) to execute liquidation, comply with outstanding obligations, and close operation in country
  • After CNAD-set window expires → CNAD orders ex officio cancellation of registration; all rights extinguished
For business decision / market exit / corporate restructuring. RPSAD Art. 39(c)

The Representante Legal must be domiciled in El Salvador and is typically a local lawyer under a notarised power of attorney. See Personnel for best-practice guidance on CO appointment.
Can a single shareholder incorporate the DASP entity?
No. LEAD requires an S.A. de C.V. or a foreign branch LEAD Art. 18(d), and an S.A. de C.V. requires a minimum of two shareholders Cód. Com. Art. 192. Single-shareholder forms (S.A.S.) are excluded. Typical structures use a holding company plus a nominal second shareholder with 1% for quorum purposes.
How long does the full process take in practice?
Eight to twelve months end-to-end practice: 4–6 weeks entity setup, 8–16 weeks documentation, 12–18 weeks CNAD review (two to three rounds), plus payment and registration seat. See the Flow timeline.
What does registration actually cost, all-in?
Government fee is fixed at $6,132 LEAD Art. 12 plus $4,088 annual renewal. Three realistic scenarios practice: Lean ≈ $70–110k year 1 (in-house legal, fractional CO, entry-tier KYT); Standard ≈ $115–185k year 1 (external counsel, full-time CO, mid-tier KYT — most common); Heavy ≈ $185–280k year 1 (CO + Deputy + CISO + premium tools). Year 2+ ongoing drops 20–30% as launch costs roll off. Full breakdown in Costs.
Which digital assets may a DASP offer?
Major transparent-ledger assets (BTC, ETH, SOL, TRX and similar) and CNAD-authorised stablecoins (USDT, USDC). Privacy coins are prohibited in practice (CNAD operational position based on AML/CTF risk); algorithmic stablecoins are excluded under the Stablecoin Regulation; additional stablecoins and issued tokens require a separate CNAD analysis under the Stablecoin / Issuer Regulations. See Asset Perimeter.
Can Salvadoran banks service a DASP?
Local retail banks cover operational expenses (rent, payroll, government fees) but do not, in practice, service international digital-asset flows practice. Client fiat flows typically route through foreign correspondent banks, EMIs or stablecoin rails. A realistic banking strategy is part of the business plan reviewed by CNAD.
Is a DASP licence portable — can it be passported to the EU or the US?
No. The DASP registration is national. Passporting regimes such as MiCA (EU) and state-by-state MTLs (US) are independent frameworks. Clients served in other jurisdictions must be supported under those jurisdictions' rules or through local partners.
Do I need a separate BSP registration if I also handle Bitcoin?
Yes. BSP (Bitcoin Service Provider) is a distinct registration with the BCR Registry under the Bitcoin Law (D-57/2021 as reformed by D-199/2025), Reglamento D-27/2021. SSF supervises ongoing operations. AML/CFT obligations flow to UIF (same as DASP). The 2025 Reform changed retail Bitcoin acceptance (now voluntary, private actors only) but the BSP framework itself is unchanged. Entities that handle BTC in addition to other digital assets hold both licences in parallel. See BSP vs DASP.
What happens if the CEO, a director or the board changes post-registration?
Submit a modification-of-registration application to CNAD RPSAD Art. 13 — RPSAD does not set a fixed entity deadline; CNAD responds within 20 working days. Separately, if the Compliance Officer or a UBO changes, the entity's UIF registration data must be kept updated — changes notified within 15 working days Instr. Art. 3. See Ongoing Obligations.
Can a Salvadoran bank or other SSF-supervised entity register as a DASP?
Yes. RPSAD Art. 47 explicitly allows entities supervised by the Superintendency of the Financial System (SSF) — banks, insurance companies, broker-dealers, fund administrators, EMIs — to request registration as DASPs, provided they fully comply with LEAD and RPSAD. They keep their SSF supervision for their core business and add CNAD supervision for the DASP scope. In practice, only a handful of Salvadoran banks have explored this path so far; pure-play DASP entities incorporated as S.A. de C.V. remain the dominant model.
How does CNAD send official notifications?
CNAD uses the email address registered by the Service Provider in its registration application. Notifications by technical means are deemed effective 24 hours after sending, provided there is evidence of dispatch to the registered email RPSAD Art. 49. The system generates an automatic electronic notification record (sent / received timestamps). Operational discipline: the registered notification mailbox must be monitored daily; CNAD timelines run from the deemed-received moment, not from human acknowledgement.
How many meetings with CNAD does the registration process involve?
Four meetings: (1) Phase 1 — Technical (CNAD): confirm availability within 5 bd, held within 10 bd of form submission CNAD Procedure; (2) Phase 2 — Technical platform deep-dive (CNAD): same 5 bd / 10 bd timing; non-attendance at either triggers archive of the application LPA Art. 72; (3) Phase 4 — Inscription Google Meet (CNAD): platform validation prior to definitive registration CNAD Guía § Inscripción; (4) Phase 5 — Pre-operations virtual meeting with the CNAD Consejo Directivo, the DASP's senior management, shareholders and Board members, after favourable resolution and before commencement of operations CNAD-CD-132-2026. See the Detailed phase view.

Glossary

Abbreviations, Spanish terms and recurring concepts used throughout the guide.

AML / CFT
Anti-money laundering / counter-financing of terrorism.
bd
Business day. Working days under Salvadoran public administration calendar.
BCR
Banco Central de Reserva de El Salvador. Maintains the BSP Registry (Bitcoin track per D-27/2021 Art. 3). Receives BSP registration applications, pre-operations documentation, registry-update notifications and discontinuation notices — but not ongoing operational AML reports (those go to UIF). Ongoing supervision of BSPs is delegated to the SSF (D-27/2021 Art. 5).
BO / UBO
Beneficial Owner / Ultimate Beneficial Owner — the natural person who ultimately owns or controls the client. Statutory thresholds: ≥10% capital / voting rights or control by other means [Instr. Arts. 12 last sentence, 20(b)] · ≥25% [LCLDA Art. 15 par. 5] · majority + control catch-all + highest-ranking management fallback [Instr. Art. 21-A Reform 2]. Apply 10% as floor (stricter rule). Independent of %: also UBO is the natural person on whose behalf the relationship is sought (nominee detection) [Instr. Art. 20(a)]. For trusts: identify all parties — settlor, trustee, protector, beneficiaries, controllers [Instr. Arts. 21, 21-A]. Listed-company exception applies if subject to disclosure regime + adequate UBO transparency.
BSP
Bitcoin Service Provider. Registration under the Bitcoin Law (Decree 57/2021, as reformed by Decree 199/2025) + Reglamento (Decree 27/2021). The 2025 Reform affected retail Bitcoin acceptance (now voluntary, private actors only; State no longer accepts BTC for taxes; Chivo wallet wound down) but did NOT affect the BSP registration framework — BCR Registry, SSF supervision, AML obligations all unchanged.
CDD
Customer Due Diligence — measures in Instr. Art. 12 (a–i). Triggered by ongoing relationship [LCLDA Art. 15(a)] OR occasional transaction at or above the applicable reporting threshold for the transaction type [LCLDA Art. 15(b) → Instr. Arts. 51, 52]: $1,000 e-transfer (Art. 52(a)(b)) · $200 remittance (Art. 52(c)) · $10K cash (Art. 51) · $25K other means (Art. 51). STR-related suspicion triggers full CDD irrespective of amount.
CNAD
Comisión Nacional de Activos Digitales. The digital-asset regulator.
CNAD Guide (deprecated)
The CNAD AML/CFT Risk Management Guide for the Digital Assets Industry (2023) cited Instructivo Art. 84-B(1)(e), which has been restructured by Reform (2) into Art. 52 par. 2. References to the Guide have been removed across this portal; binding sources are Instructivo Art. 52 reformed, LCLDA Art. 15, LCLDA and LECAT.
CNR
Centro Nacional de Registros. Corporate and commercial registry.
CO
Compliance Officer. Principal and Alternate COs are mandatory for DASPs [Instr. Art. 84-B(2) Reform 1]. Both may be foreign-resident with an employment relationship to the entity; best practice: Salvadoran-resident Principal CO for smoother CNAD review.
Cód. Com.
Código de Comercio — Salvadoran Commercial Code.
CTR
Currency Transaction Report. Used internationally for threshold reports; the Salvadoran statutory equivalent is Operación Regulada [Instr. Art. 51].
LCLDA
Ley Contra el Lavado de Dinero y Activos — Decreto Legislativo 426/2025 — the current AML/CFT/CFP Law. Replaces D-498/1998. Promulgated 8 October 2025; vigencia 8 days after D.O. publication [Art. 62]. Self-references as "LCLDA" throughout the verbatim text.
DA
Digital Asset. Defined in LEAD Art. 3.
DASP
Digital Asset Service Provider. Registration under LEAD (Decree 643/2023).
DLT
Distributed Ledger Technology.
EDD
Enhanced Due Diligence [Instr. Art. 13]. Triggered by PEP, high-risk jurisdiction, opaque ownership, structuring, profile deviation, flagged wallet, sanctions-adjacent risk. Adds documentary SOF + SOW + senior-management approval to CDD.
FATF
Financial Action Task Force. R.16 = Travel Rule; R.10 = customer due diligence; R.12 = PEPs.
FIU
Financial Intelligence Unit. The Salvadoran FIU is UIF, housed at the Fiscalía General de la República.
FGR
Fiscalía General de la República — Attorney General's Office; hosts the UIF.
Instr. UIF
Instructivo UIF — Acuerdo 380/2021. Operational AML instructions; still in force transitionally under LCLDA Art. 61 "in what does not contradict the Law".
IVA
Impuesto al Valor Agregado — value-added tax (13%).
KYB
Know Your Business. CDD applied to corporate clients.
KYC
Know Your Customer.
KYT
Know Your Transaction. Ongoing transaction-level monitoring, blockchain-trace-based for DA operations.
LaaS
Legal-as-a-Service. Retainer bundling legal rep, accounting and audit.
LEAD
Ley de Emisión de Activos Digitales. The primary DASP statute.
LECAT
D-108 — Special Law against Acts of Terrorism. UNSC + UIF national list freeze duties [Art. 37]. Tipping-off prohibition is in LCLDA Art. 44 (not LECAT).
NIT
Número de Identificación Tributaria — Salvadoran tax ID.
Operación Regulada
Threshold report (Salvadoran equivalent of CTR): cash > US$10,000 or other means > US$25,000, single or monthly aggregate, filed with UIF within 5 bd [Instr. Art. 51]. Independent from DD level and from Travel Rule.
PEP
Politically Exposed Person — self, family ≤ 2nd degree and close associates [Instr. Arts. 16 (national PEPs) / 17 (foreign PEPs) / 18 (international-organisation PEPs)]. PEP status persists 5 years post-cease. Always EDD + senior-management approval + documentary SOF + SOW [LCLDA Art. 16 + Instr. Art. 13].
RID
Registry of Issuers of Digital Assets — issuer-side registration.
RPSAD
Reglamento para Proveedores de Servicios de Activos Digitales — CNAD-035-2023.
S.A. de C.V.
Sociedad Anónima de Capital Variable. The permitted DASP corporate form.
S.A.S.
Sociedad por Acciones Simplificada — simplified form, excluded for DASPs.
SDD
Simplified Due Diligence — practice-level track within Instr. Art. 12 for documented-low-risk occasional transactions < US$1,000. Still requires basic identification, sanctions screening and Travel Rule.
SOF
Source of Funds — origin of the specific monies used in the operation. Declarative at CDD baseline; documentary under EDD / PEP.
SOW
Source of Wealth — origin of the client's overall assets (lifetime narrative). NOT required at CDD; documentary under EDD, PEP or material profile deviation.
STR
Suspicious Transaction Report (sometimes SAR). Analysis ≤ 15 bd, filing 24 h after determination [LCLDA Art. 24]. Attempted operations also reportable [Instr. Art. 47]. Tipping-off prohibited [LCLDA Art. 44].
Travel Rule
Per-transfer originator + beneficiary data requirement for every electronic transfer regardless of amount [Instr. Art. 52 par. 2 reformed]. 7 mandatory data fields (originator name, account, ID type+number; beneficiary name, account, beneficiary bank name; transaction ID). Plus batch-processing requirement for grouped transfers. See full breakdown in the Compliance guide.
UIF
Unidad de Investigación Financiera — the Salvadoran FIU, at FGR.
UNSC
United Nations Security Council. The 1267 / 1373 sanctions consolidated list is directly binding via LECAT Art. 37.
