Why El Salvador

Understanding the El Salvador DASP License

El Salvador was the first country to adopt a dedicated digital-asset statute (LEAD, 2023) alongside Bitcoin legal tender (2021). The regime is designed for serious operators — USD-denominated, low-tax, AML-aligned to FATF but lighter than the EU's MiCA, and explicitly open to stablecoins and foreign compliance staff. The combination is unique in the Americas.

01

Zero-tax regime

0% income, 0% capital gains, 0% VAT, 0% municipal on DASP-scope activity — effective from day 1 of registration LEAD Art. 36.

02

Dollarized economy

USD is legal tender since 2001. No currency conversion, no exchange-control risk, no FX mismatch with client operations.

03

Direct USDT on/off-ramp

Stablecoin exchange to and from USD is explicitly legal under LEAD. No third-country routing, no correspondent-banking workaround.

04

Lighter AML than MiCA

$1,000 CDD trigger for occasional transactions; senior-mgmt approval only at EDD; no licensing capital requirements. FATF-aligned but pragmatic.

05

8–12 months to licence

Five clearly-defined CNAD phases with statutory clocks (20 bd + 10 bd per cycle). Compare to 18–24 months under MiCA or FINMA process.

06

No residency for key roles

CO, Deputy CO, Directors can all be foreign-resident — explicit exemption for DASPs Instr. Art. 84-B(2). Uncommon in LatAm.

07

Stablecoin-ready framework

CNAD has a dedicated Stablecoin Regulation with reserve rules and issuer registry — first fully-articulated stablecoin regime in the Americas.

08

BSP + DASP dual rail

Bitcoin operations under the Bitcoin Law (BSP, BCR); everything else under LEAD (DASP, CNAD). One entity, two parallel licences.

BSP vs DASP — two registries, one entity

El Salvador operates two parallel licensing rails. BSP covers Bitcoin, DASP covers everything else. Most serious operators hold both.

BSP · Bitcoin Rail

Bitcoin Service Provider

Supervisor: BCR · Primary law: Bitcoin Law (Decree 57/2021)

  • Bitcoin (BTC) only
  • AML supervisor: UIF via BCR channel
  • Registration fee set by BCR
  • BTC-specific tax regime
  • Market rate for BSP support: ~$5,085 incl. VAT
+
DASP · Digital Assets Rail

Digital Asset Service Provider

Supervisor: CNAD · Primary law: LEAD (Decree 643/2023)

  • All digital assets other than BTC
  • AML supervisor: UIF via CNAD channel
  • Registration fee: $6,132 (15 min. salaries)
  • LEAD Art. 36 — 0% income, CG, VAT, municipal
  • Market rate for DASP support: ~$5,085 incl. VAT

Exchanges and custodians handling both Bitcoin and other digital assets typically hold both licences in the same S.A. de C.V. entity. The two rails share a compliance team, AML manual and CO.

Legal Framework

Applicable Legislation

Five instruments govern DASPs in El Salvador. The binding stack is LEAD + RPSAD + D-426 (AML Law) + Instructivo UIF + LECAT. Outdated references to the pre-reform CNAD AML Guide have been removed across the portal.

LEAD — Ley de Emisión de Activos Digitales

Decree No. 643 of 9 January 2023. Primary statute for digital asset service providers, issuers and certifiers. Establishes CNAD authority, registration requirements, tax benefits and the sanctions framework.

Primary Law

RPSAD — Reglamento para Proveedores de Servicios de Activos Digitales

CNAD-035-2023. Detailed regulation for DASP registration, ongoing obligations, cybersecurity, customer service and operational rules. 48 articles.

DASP Regulation

AML Law — D-426

Decree No. 426 — Special Law for the Prevention, Control and Punishment of Asset Laundering, Financing of Terrorism, and Financing of the Proliferation of Weapons of Mass Destruction. Defines reporting entities (incl. DASPs via supervision by CNAD), CDD trigger (Art. 15), 24-hour STR (Art. 24), regulated transactions reporting (Art. 25), 15-year retention (Art. 26), tipping-off prohibition (Art. 44).

AML/CFT

Instructivo UIF (Reform 1 + Reform 2)

Operational rulebook from the Financial Investigation Unit. CDD measures (Art. 12), EDD (Art. 13), SDD (Art. 14), PEP (Arts. 16–18), beneficial ownership 10% floor (Art. 20) + UBO majority/control (Art. 21-A), Operación Regulada thresholds (Art. 51), Travel Rule data (Art. 52 par. 2 reformed), VASP-specific obligations (Art. 84-A registration, Art. 84-B general obligations, Art. 84-C reports).

UIF Guidelines

LECAT — D-108

Special Law against Acts of Terrorism. UNSC sanctions freeze obligation (Art. 37), terrorist designation before UNSC (Art. 34-B). Note: tipping-off prohibition is in D-426 Art. 44, not in LECAT.

CFT

Issuer & Stablecoin Regulations

CNAD-017-2023 (Issuer Regulation) and Stablecoin Regulation. Govern public and private issuances, RID requirements, certifier process and stablecoin reserve rules. Separate registration track from DASP — included for completeness.

Asset Perimeter

Permitted & Restricted Digital Assets

Not every digital asset is marketable under a DASP licence. Restrictions are set at registration and reinforced in every registration resolution LEAD Arts. 9(k)(l), 21(e) RPSAD Art. 41.

Permitted by default

  • Major transparent-ledger assets (BTC, ETH, SOL, TRX and similar)
  • Regulated stablecoins expressly authorised by CNAD (e.g. USDT, USDC)
  • Assets declared in the DASP registration file and accepted by CNAD

Prohibited or restricted

  • Privacy coins — prohibited. Assets with limited or non-existent traceability (Monero XMR, ZCash ZEC and similar) may not be marketed under any circumstances
  • Algorithmic stablecoins — excluded entirely
  • Additional stablecoins — require express prior CNAD authorisation
  • Tokens from issuances — must be submitted to CNAD for analysis before marketing

How restrictions are imposed. The list of permitted assets is locked at registration. Any addition requires a material change notification and, for stablecoins and issued tokens, a separate CNAD analysis. Operating outside the authorised perimeter is a sanctionable breach under LEAD Arts. 38–40.

Cross-border reach. The pre-registration form requires the applicant to declare its intended operating area LEAD Art. 18. Serving clients outside the declared perimeter — or marketing to jurisdictions where digital-asset services are prohibited — is a compliance breach. DASPs must maintain a list of restricted jurisdictions and apply geofencing and KYC filters accordingly Instr. Art. 54 CNAD Procedure §9.

Government fee $6,132 (15 min. monthly salaries LEAD Art. 12) is paid only after favourable resolution, within 10 bd, or the registration is automatically revoked RPSAD Art. 10.

CNAD Review

Application Evaluation

CNAD reviews within 20 bd; if observations are raised, the applicant has 10 bd to respond. Most applications complete two to three review rounds before approval.

20 bd per round
▾
  • CNAD review: 20 business days RPSAD Art. 8 LEAD Art. 20
  • If incomplete: applicant has 10 business days to cure RPSAD Art. 8
  • Each significant change: +5 business days RPSAD Art. 9
  • CNAD may extend any term by up to 50% RPSAD Art. 44
  • Technical presentation during review: platform, DLT, security, KYC CNAD Procedure

Timeline math: one round ≈ 20 bd (CNAD) + 10 bd (applicant) ≈ 6 calendar weeks. With two to three rounds: 12–18 weeks practice.

UIF Notification

CNAD notifies the Financial Investigation Unit of the new DASP registration.

▾
  • Commission notifies UIF of new registered DASP CNAD Procedure

Cure periods — conflict of norms. LEAD Art. 20 grants 5 business days to cure; RPSAD Art. 8 grants 10 working days. RPSAD is the more recent and more specific rule — in practice, the 10-day period applies.

CNAD may also request further information before the 20-day review deadline expires, setting a custom term RPSAD Art. 8. Any statutory term may be extended by up to 50% RPSAD Art. 44.

Ongoing Obligations

Recurring duties that survive registration. Non-compliance triggers sanctions and, in the case of unpaid renewal, automatic loss of registration and tax benefits.

Annual Renewal

  • Fee: $4,088 (10 × $408.80), due in Q1 each year RPSAD Art. 12
  • Non-payment → registration cancelled and tax benefits lost RPSAD Art. 12

Quarterly Reporting

  • Operational and financial report each quarter RPSAD Arts. 15–16
  • Submission window: the first 20 days of April, July, October and January

Material Changes — CNAD

  • Notify CNAD within 5 business days of any significant change to registration documents RPSAD Art. 9
  • Covers CEO, board members and organisational structure RPSAD Art. 7(b)(ii), 7(e)

Material Changes — UIF

  • CO or UBO changes require UIF update within 15 business days Instr. Art. 3 Instr. Art. 64
  • Other data (name, NIT, address) on the same 15-day cadence

Records Retention

  • All AML records kept for 15 years D-426 Art. 26
  • Applies to CDD files, transaction records, STRs and training logs

Code of Ethics

  • Must be adopted within 6 months of registration RPSAD Art. 18
  • Covers conflicts of interest, confidentiality and treatment of clients

Entity

Local Company Requirements

Two corporate forms qualify: a Salvadoran S.A. de C.V. or a registered branch of a foreign company LEAD Art. 18(d). Simplified forms (S.A.S.) and limited liability companies are excluded.

Incorporation

  • S.A. de C.V. or foreign branch only LEAD Art. 18(d)
  • Minimum 2 shareholders Cód. Com. Art. 192 — S.A.S. excluded for DASPs
  • Notarised deed + CNR registration RPSAD Art. 7(b)(i)
  • NIT required RPSAD Art. 7(b)(iii)
  • Fiscal domicile in El Salvador (virtual office accepted)
  • Share capital: $2,000 nominal, 5% paid at inception

UIF Registration

  • Entity registered on the UIF platform D-426 Art. 9(1)
  • Supervisor assignment: CNAD for non-BTC DASPs, BCR for BTC Instr. Art. 84-A
  • UBO information required Instr. Art. 3(2)
  • Registered data kept current — changes notified within 15 bd Instr. Art. 3

Banking & Accounting

  • Local bank account for operational needs (government fees, payroll, rent)
  • Accounting in Spanish, USD as functional currency
  • IFRS for SMEs, annual external audit
  • Records retained 15 years D-426 Art. 26

Banking reality check. Salvadoran retail banks do not, in practice, service international digital-asset flows. The local account covers operating expenses only; client-fund custody, fiat on/off-ramps and settlement are typically routed through foreign correspondent banks, EMIs or stablecoin rails practice. A realistic banking strategy is part of the business plan reviewed by CNAD.

No "registered agent" concept for S.A. de C.V. El Salvador uses a civil-law corporate model: the company is represented by its Administrador Único or Junta Directiva and a separate Representante Legal (local resident). There is no US-style registered-agent service. Foreign branches must appoint a Salvadoran-resident legal representative via notarised power of attorney LEAD Art. 18(d).

Incentives

Tax Benefits LEAD Art. 36

LEAD Art. 36 grants a zero-tax regime for DASP activities listed in Art. 19. Benefits are conditional on active registration and apply from provisional registration onward.

0%Income Tax Art. 36(b)(c)
0%Capital Gains Art. 36(b)
0%VAT (IVA) Art. 36(c)
0%Municipal Tax Art. 36(c)
✓Shareholder Pass-Through Art. 36(d)
Day 1From Registration RPSAD Art. 11

Scope limits. Exemptions apply only to income and supplies tied to Art. 19 DASP activities. Ancillary income outside Art. 19 — including exchange for goods and services — falls under the ordinary tax regime Art. 36(e).

Conditionality. Benefits are tied to a live registration. Non-renewal or cancellation results in automatic loss of benefits RPSAD Art. 12. There is no grandfathering for past periods.

What drives the variance. The biggest swing factors are (i) compliance headcount — full-time CO ($2.5–4k/mo in El Salvador market) vs. fractional CO services ($1–1.8k/mo); (ii) KYT tier — entry-level ($500–1,200/mo) vs. enterprise ($2.5–5k/mo); (iii) policy pack origin — in-house template-driven ($0–3k one-time) vs. external counsel ($8–15k); (iv) independent AML audit firm tier ($3–6k Big-4 light vs. $8–15k full).

Government fees ($6,132 + $4,088 annual) are statutory and do not vary. Capital deposit ($2,000 minimum) is balance-sheet capital, not a sunk cost — recovered on liquidation.

Frequently Asked

FAQ

The questions foreign founders ask most often during intake. Each answer links to the relevant section or regulation.

Do shareholders, directors or COs need to be Salvadoran residents?
No residency is required for shareholders, directors (Administrador/Board) or DASP Compliance Officers Instr. Art. 84-B(2). Only the Representante Legal must be a Salvadoran resident, and this role can be held by a local lawyer under a notarised power of attorney. See Personnel.
Can a single shareholder incorporate the DASP entity?
No. LEAD requires an S.A. de C.V. or a foreign branch LEAD Art. 18(d), and an S.A. de C.V. requires a minimum of two shareholders Cód. Com. Art. 192. Single-shareholder forms (S.A.S.) are excluded. Typical structures use a holding company plus a nominal second shareholder with 1% for quorum purposes.
How long does the full process take in practice?
Eight to twelve months end-to-end practice: 4–6 weeks entity setup, 8–16 weeks documentation, 12–18 weeks CNAD review (two to three rounds), plus payment and registration seat. See the Flow timeline.
What does registration actually cost, all-in?
Government fee is fixed at $6,132 LEAD Art. 12 plus $4,088 annual renewal. Three realistic scenarios practice: Lean ≈ $70–110k year 1 (in-house legal, fractional CO, entry-tier KYT); Standard ≈ $115–185k year 1 (external counsel, full-time CO, mid-tier KYT — most common); Heavy ≈ $185–280k year 1 (CO + Deputy + CISO + premium tools). Year 2+ ongoing drops 20–30% as launch costs roll off. Full breakdown in Costs.
Which digital assets may a DASP offer?
Major transparent-ledger assets (BTC, ETH, SOL, TRX and similar) and CNAD-authorised stablecoins (USDT, USDC). Privacy coins are prohibited; algorithmic stablecoins are excluded; additional stablecoins and issued tokens require a separate CNAD analysis RPSAD Art. 41. See Asset Perimeter.
Can Salvadoran banks service a DASP?
Local retail banks cover operational expenses (rent, payroll, government fees) but do not, in practice, service international digital-asset flows practice. Client fiat flows typically route through foreign correspondent banks, EMIs or stablecoin rails. A realistic banking strategy is part of the business plan reviewed by CNAD.
Is a DASP licence portable — can it be passported to the EU or the US?
No. The DASP registration is national. Passporting regimes such as MiCA (EU) and state-by-state MTLs (US) are independent frameworks. Clients served in other jurisdictions must be supported under those jurisdictions' rules or through local partners.
Do I need a separate BSP registration if I also handle Bitcoin?
Yes. BSP (Bitcoin Service Provider) is a distinct registration supervised by the BCR under the Bitcoin Law (Decree 57/2021). Entities that handle BTC in addition to other digital assets hold both licences in parallel. See BSP vs DASP.
What happens if the CEO, a director or the board changes post-registration?
Notify CNAD within 5 business days RPSAD Art. 9. Separately, if the Compliance Officer or a UBO changes, update the UIF within 15 business days Instr. Art. 3. See Ongoing Obligations.
Does CNAD meet the applicant in person at any stage?
A technical presentation may be scheduled during the documentation review CNAD Procedure. In addition, after registration is granted, CNAD holds a virtual meeting with senior management, shareholders and the Board of Commissioners to align on commencement of operations CNAD Procedure.

Glossary

Abbreviations, Spanish terms and recurring concepts used throughout the guide.

AML / CFT
Anti-money laundering / counter-financing of terrorism.
bd
Business day. Working days under Salvadoran public administration calendar.
BCR
Banco Central de Reserva de El Salvador. Supervises BSPs.
BO / UBO
Beneficial Owner / Ultimate Beneficial Owner — the natural person who ultimately owns or controls the client. Statutory thresholds: ≥10% [Instr. Arts. 12, 20], ≥25% [D-426 Art. 2]. Apply 10% as floor (stricter rule). For trusts, identify all parties [Instr. Art. 21].
BSP
Bitcoin Service Provider. Registration under the Bitcoin Law (Decree 57/2021).
CDD
Customer Due Diligence — measures in Instr. Art. 12 (a–i). Triggered by ongoing relationship [D-426 Art. 15(a)] OR occasional transaction above the applicable reporting threshold for that transaction type [D-426 Art. 15(b) → Instr. Arts. 51, 52]. For DASPs handling e-transfers, the practical CDD trigger is US,000 (Art. 52(a)(b)).
CNAD
Comisión Nacional de Activos Digitales. The digital-asset regulator.
CNAD Guide (deprecated)
The CNAD AML/CFT Risk Management Guide for the Digital Assets Industry (2023) cited Instructivo Art. 84-B(1)(e), which has been restructured by Reform (2) into Art. 52 par. 2. References to the Guide have been removed across this portal; binding sources are Instructivo Art. 52 reformed, D-426 Art. 15, D-426 and LECAT.
CNR
Centro Nacional de Registros. Corporate and commercial registry.
CO
Compliance Officer. Principal and Alternate COs are mandatory for DASPs.
Cód. Com.
Código de Comercio — Salvadoran Commercial Code.
CTR
Currency Transaction Report. Used internationally for threshold reports; the Salvadoran statutory equivalent is Operación Regulada [Instr. Art. 51].
D-426
Decreto Legislativo 426/2025 — the current AML Law, replaces D-498/1998. Promulgated 8 October 2025; vigencia 8 days after D.O. publication [Art. 62].
DA
Digital Asset. Defined in LEAD Art. 3.
DASP
Digital Asset Service Provider. Registration under LEAD (Decree 643/2023).
DLT
Distributed Ledger Technology.
EDD
Enhanced Due Diligence [Instr. Art. 13]. Triggered by PEP, high-risk jurisdiction, opaque ownership, structuring, profile deviation, flagged wallet, sanctions-adjacent risk. Adds documentary SOF + SOW + senior-management approval to CDD.
FATF
Financial Action Task Force. R.16 = Travel Rule; R.10 = customer due diligence; R.12 = PEPs.
FIU
Financial Intelligence Unit. The Salvadoran FIU is UIF, housed at the Fiscalía General de la República.
FGR
Fiscalía General de la República — Attorney General's Office; hosts the UIF.
Instr. UIF
Instructivo UIF — Acuerdo 380/2021. Operational AML instructions; still in force transitionally under D-426 Art. 61 "in what does not contradict the Law".
IVA
Impuesto al Valor Agregado — value-added tax (13%).
KYB
Know Your Business. CDD applied to corporate clients.
KYC
Know Your Customer.
KYT
Know Your Transaction. Ongoing transaction-level monitoring, blockchain-trace-based for DA operations.
LaaS
Legal-as-a-Service. Retainer bundling legal rep, accounting and audit.
LEAD
Ley de Emisión de Activos Digitales. The primary DASP statute.
LECAT
Ley Especial Contra Actos de Terrorismo. Source of UNSC + UIF-national-list freeze duties [Art. 37] and tipping-off prohibition [Art. 17].
NIT
Número de Identificación Tributaria — Salvadoran tax ID.
Operación Regulada
Threshold report (Salvadoran equivalent of CTR): cash > US$10,000 or other means > US$25,000, single or monthly aggregate, filed with UIF within 5 bd [Instr. Art. 51]. Independent from DD level and from Travel Rule.
PEP
Politically Exposed Person — self, family ≤ 2nd degree and close associates [Instr. Arts. 14, 16–18]. PEP status persists 5 years post-cease. Always EDD + senior-management approval + documentary SOF + SOW.
RID
Registry of Issuers of Digital Assets — issuer-side registration.
RPSAD
Reglamento para Proveedores de Servicios de Activos Digitales — CNAD-035-2023.
S.A. de C.V.
Sociedad Anónima de Capital Variable. The permitted DASP corporate form.
S.A.S.
Sociedad por Acciones Simplificada — simplified form, excluded for DASPs.
SDD
Simplified Due Diligence — practice-level track within Instr. Art. 12 for documented-low-risk occasional transactions < US$1,000. Still requires basic identification, sanctions screening and Travel Rule.
SOF
Source of Funds — origin of the specific monies used in the operation. Declarative at CDD baseline; documentary under EDD / PEP.
SOW
Source of Wealth — origin of the client's overall assets (lifetime narrative). NOT required at CDD; documentary under EDD, PEP or material profile deviation.
STR
Suspicious Transaction Report (sometimes SAR). Analysis ≤ 15 bd, filing 24 h after determination [D-426 Art. 24]. Attempted operations also reportable [Instr. Art. 47]. Tipping-off prohibited [LECAT Art. 17].
Travel Rule
Per-transfer originator + beneficiary data requirement for every electronic transfer regardless of amount [Instr. Art. 52 par. 2 reformed]. 7 mandatory data fields (originator name, account, ID type+number; beneficiary name, account, beneficiary bank name; transaction ID). Plus batch-processing requirement for grouped transfers. See full breakdown in the Compliance guide.
UIF
Unidad de Investigación Financiera — the Salvadoran FIU, at FGR.
UNSC
United Nations Security Council. The 1267 / 1373 sanctions consolidated list is directly binding via LECAT Art. 37.
