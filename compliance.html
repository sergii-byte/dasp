Regulatory stack

Binding sources, in priority order

Every citation in this guide resolves to one of the five sources below. References that previously pointed to the CNAD AML Guide (deprecated) have been replaced with the underlying statute or the reformed Instructivo.

D-426
AML / CTF Law (statute)

Special Law for the Prevention, Control and Punishment of Asset Laundering, Financing of Terrorism, and Financing of the Proliferation of Weapons of Mass Destruction. Reporting entities (Art. 7), CDD trigger (Art. 15), PEP definitions (Art. 19), CO and Prevention Committee (Arts. 20, 23), STR 24h (Art. 24), regulated transactions (Art. 25), 15-yr retention (Art. 26), tipping-off (Art. 44).

Instr.
Instructivo UIF (Reform 1 + Reform 2)

Operational rulebook from the Financial Investigation Unit. Standard CDD with identity verification (Art. 12 Reform 2), EDD (Art. 13 Reform 2), SDD (Art. 14), PEP enhanced measures (Arts. 16–18), beneficial ownership 10% floor (Art. 20) + UBO majority control (Art. 21-A), Operación Regulada thresholds (Art. 51), Travel Rule data (Art. 52 par. 2 Reform 2), VASP-specific obligations (Arts. 84-A, 84-B, 84-C — Reform 1).

LECAT
D-108 — Counter-terrorism law

UNSC sanctions freeze obligation (Art. 37). Designation of terrorists before the UNSC (Art. 34-B). Note: tipping-off is in D-426 Art. 44, not in LECAT.

RPSAD
DASP Regulation (CNAD)

CNAD-035-2023. Day-to-day DASP rules: registration (Arts. 7–11), governance, customer service (Art. 22), risk management (Art. 21), cybersecurity (Arts. 25–26).

LEAD
Decree 643/2023 — DASP statute

Establishes CNAD authority, DASP registration regime (Arts. 18–21), permitted activities (Art. 19), tax exemption (Art. 36), registration fee schedule (Art. 12).

Customer Due Diligence

CDD by trigger — what activates, when

El Salvador anchors the CDD trigger in D-426 Art. 15. Two independent paths trigger full CDD: an ongoing relationship, or an occasional transaction above the applicable reporting threshold for that channel. EDD is a separate layer — triggered by risk factors regardless of amount.

The four parallel triggers

Most DASP operations are e-transfers — the practical CDD trigger is therefore $1,000 via the D-426 Art. 15(b) → Instr. Art. 52(a) chain. Internal AML policy may calibrate a lower threshold per FATF R.10 risk-based principles.

All three sit on top of a baseline of Travel Rule data + sanctions screening which applies to every transfer regardless of DD level.

Identification & verification

Identity verification — the gating step

Reform 2 of the Instructivo added a binding sequence: identity must be verified BEFORE the commercial relationship begins, via official identification documents issued by competent public authorities. If verification cannot be completed, the relationship cannot start — and the failure is itself analyzed under the suspicious-transaction framework.

Natural person — minimum data set

Instr. Art. 12(a) + identity-verification clause (Reform 2)

  • Full name as on official document
  • Type and number of ID (DUI, passport, residence card)
  • Date of birth
  • Address (utility ≤3 months / bank statement / registry)
  • Occupation (real economic activity, not just DUI/NIT data)
  • Tax ID (NIT) if applicable
  • PEP self-declaration + screening
Verification before relationship: official document must be current at onboarding. Instr. Art. 12(a) Reform 2
Legal entity — minimum data set

Instr. Art. 12 + Reform 2 identity clause + Art. 20 BO chain

  • Corporate name + commercial name
  • Principal address in country
  • Articles of incorporation + amendments — registered with the Commercial Register
  • Powers binding the legal person + senior management names
  • NIT + operating licence
  • Nature of main activity (real, beyond registry classification)
  • Beneficial owner chain to natural person (≥10% floor)
  • Authorised signatories with IDs and powers
Identity must be verified BEFORE relationship begins. Instr. Art. 12(a) Reform 2

Method & channel

  • In person OR remote via digital / electronic channels, after a documented risk analysis of the chosen channel Instr. Art. 12(a)
  • The identification document must be current at the time of onboarding
  • The economic activity declared on the DUI / NIT is not sufficient — further enquiry is required to determine the actual activity in the real economy
  • For legal persons: identification reaches the ownership structure and ultimate beneficial owner, not just the corporate counterparty
  • Trustees must disclose their status when establishing a relationship; identification of all parties to the trust is required Instr. Arts. 21, 21-A

If identity cannot be verified. The commercial relationship may not be commenced. The Reporting Entity must analyze the circumstances under the unusual / suspicious framework (Instructivo Title II Chapters VII–VIII) and consider filing an attempted suspicious-transaction report. Instr. Art. 12 Reform 2 · Instr. Art. 47

Customer profile (post-identity)

Once identity is verified, build a customer profile via interview sheet and a sworn statement, defining the economic activity. The sworn statement is per customer, not per product. Instr. Art. 12(b)

List checks at identification — verbatim per Instr. Art. 12

  • (e) Lists of natural / legal persons involved in ML / TF / FPWMD offences, issued by countries or international bodies binding on El Salvador Instr. Art. 12(e) · operational anchor for UNSC matches → freeze obligation LECAT Art. 37
  • (f) Lists of jurisdictions classified as zero / low-taxation or as tax havens; persons linked to criminal acts Instr. Art. 12(f)
  • (g) Lists of natural persons performing or having performed prominent public functions (PEPs) — domestic and foreign Instr. Art. 12(g) · PEP-specific EDD measures in Instr. Arts. 16–18

Beneficial owner

Resolve to a natural person — apply 10% as floor

El Salvador uses a stricter-of-three approach for beneficial ownership. Always identify and verify the natural person at the top of the chain.

10% floor

Apply this as operational baseline

Natural person who directly or indirectly owns or controls ≥10% of capital or voting rights of a legal person.

Instr. Arts. 12(a), 20
25% (statute)

D-426 statutory threshold

Statute uses 25% as the participation threshold; the Instructivo lowers it to 10% for operational rigor. Apply the lower (10%) as floor.

D-426 Art. 15
Majority shareholding / control by other means

UBO catch-all (Reform 2)

Where no natural person reaches the threshold via shareholding, identify whoever exercises control by other means; failing that, the highest-ranking management officer.

Instr. Art. 21-A (Reform 2)

Trusts and other legal arrangements. Identify all parties: settlor and whoever controls the settlor; trustee(s); protector (if any); beneficiaries (or classes); any natural person exercising effective ultimate control over the trust through any control / ownership chain. Trustees retain this information for at least 15 years after the cessation of the relationship. Instr. Arts. 21, 21-A

Refresh triggers — out of cycle

Salvadoran statute does not enumerate specific BO-refresh triggers, but Instr. Art. 23 requires that registered customer / counterparty data be kept updated and that update procedures be documented. The list below reflects FATF R.10 industry practice that operationally satisfies Art. 23.

  • Change in ownership ≥10%
  • Change in authorised signatories
  • Trust party change / nominee change
  • Beneficial owner becomes a PEP
  • Sanctions list update naming a related party
  • Material deviation from declared activity profile

Scheduled refresh cadence (industry practice — not statutory): Standard CDD every 3 years · EDD annually · half-yearly when residual risk is very high. Instr. Art. 23 requires updates "within 15 working days" of changes notified by the customer.

Travel Rule

Per-transfer data — Art. 52 par. 2 reformed

El Salvador's Travel Rule for DASPs rests on a two-article anchor: Instr. Art. 84-B(1)(e) (Reform 1) sets the VASP-specific principle (records that allow knowledge of the origin and destination of virtual-asset transactions); Instr. Art. 52 par. 2 (Reform 2) specifies the seven mandatory fields for every electronic transfer, even those that do not exceed the established thresholds, and adds a batch-processing rule.

Legal nuance — why the dual anchor matters. Art. 52 par. 2 is in Chapter IX ("Reporting of Cash and Other-Means Operations") and addresses "financial institutions". Art. 84 (Chapter IV-B, Reform 1) is the dedicated VASP chapter. The operational rule is to apply Art. 84-B(1)(e) through the Art. 52 par. 2 field set: VASPs collect the seven fields below as the baseline that satisfies "origin and destination knowledge". A supervisor reading both articles will expect to see this layered citation in the AML manual.

The data set — verbatim per Art. 52 par. 2

The statute lists exactly seven items. The text does not distinguish between natural persons and legal entities — the same fields apply to both, only the format of identifiers differs. The matrix below shows what each field looks like for each combination.

Statutory item Natural-person originator / beneficiary Legal-entity originator / beneficiary
(a) Name of the originating customer Full legal name as on government ID Corporate name (denominación social) as registered
(b) Account number of the originating customer Bank account number / wallet account ID Same — corporate bank account or platform account ID
(c) Type and number of identification document of the originating customer DUI · passport · residence card NIT · Mercantile Registry number (matrícula)
(d) Name of the beneficiary of the transfer Full legal name as declared by the originator Corporate name as declared by the originator
(e) Account number of the beneficiary of the transfer Beneficiary's bank account / wallet ID Same — corporate bank account or platform account ID
(f) Name of the bank administering the beneficiary's account Bank or VASP holding the beneficiary's account. Cannot be populated for an unhosted (self-hosted) wallet — document the limitation and apply risk-based EDD.
(g) Transaction identification number Internal reference number, on-chain transaction hash, or unique platform identifier

Note what the statute does not require: beneficiary's identification document, beneficiary's address, originator's address, transaction date, asset type / quantity, exchange rate, sending or receiving on-chain addresses. These are operationally collected by most DASPs (for KYT, sanctions screening, accounting), but they are not Travel Rule fields under Art. 52 par. 2. Adding fields beyond the statutory minimum is a risk-based decision documented in the AML manual.

Source verbatim. Instr. VASP-specific anchor: Instr. Art. 84-B(1)(e) (Reform 1).

Batch processing. When several individual electronic transfers from a single originator are grouped in one batch file for transmission to multiple beneficiaries, the file must contain the required and accurate originator information and the complete beneficiary information for each transfer, under the systems determined by UIF. Instr. Art. 52 par. 2 final

Operational obligations by VASP role

A DASP can occupy three roles on the same transfer chain — sometimes simultaneously. Each role triggers its own duties at the moment the transfer enters the order book.

Originating DASP
  1. Verify originator (CDD if triggered, sanctions screen always)
  2. Attribute originating wallet to the verified client
  3. Collect Travel Rule data set
  4. Screen beneficiary against UNSC + UIF national lists
  5. Transmit data securely to beneficiary VASP
  6. Retain full record 15 years
Beneficiary DASP
  1. Verify beneficiary (CDD if triggered)
  2. Attribute beneficiary wallet to the verified client
  3. Receive and validate Travel Rule data
  4. Flag missing fields; hold transfer pending data
  5. Screen originator against UNSC + UIF national lists
  6. Retain and monitor; STR if pattern emerges
Intermediary DASP
  1. Pass Travel Rule data unchanged forward
  2. Validate completeness on receipt
  3. Refuse to forward if fields missing
  4. Screen both originator and beneficiary
  5. Apply transaction monitoring at the routing point
  6. Retain 15 years even with no client relationship

Wallet types — hosted vs unhosted (self-hosted)

Salvadoran statute (D-426 / Instructivo / RPSAD / LEAD) does not define hosted vs unhosted wallets. The distinction comes from FATF R.16 operational guidance and is the global industry standard for Travel Rule implementation. Both wallet types fall under the same Art. 52 par. 2 obligation — but the operational handling differs.

Hosted wallet (custodial)

Counterparty is a regulated VASP holding keys on the client's behalf

  • Counterparty data received from / transmitted to the counterparty VASP via secure channel (Travel Rule protocol)
  • Counterparty VASP is identifiable; due-diligence on the VASP itself is operationally feasible
  • Reciprocal Travel Rule data exchange expected on every transfer
  • Standard CDD on the client; sanctions screen on both parties
Use Travel Rule message protocols (TRP, OpenVASP, IVMS-101 standardised data structures) where the counterparty VASP supports them.
Unhosted / self-hosted wallet

Counterparty key is held by the end-user themselves (no intermediary VASP)

  • No counterparty VASP exists to transmit Travel Rule data — the client self-attests beneficiary information
  • Client must declare beneficiary name + identifier; receiving address is the wallet itself
  • No statutory exemption in El Salvador — the Art. 52 par. 2 obligation applies in full
  • Wallet attribution required: prove the client controls the receiving wallet (Satoshi test / micro-deposit / signed message / KYT-vendor attribution)
  • Apply risk-based EDD with documented rationale on file (per FATF R.16 guidance)
  • Repeated unhosted transfers → relationship review under risk-based approach
Document the attribution method in the AML manual; apply consistently; produce an audit trail per transfer.

Wallet attribution is the operational pinch point. Art. 52 par. 2 requires originator and beneficiary identity but does not specify a wallet-attribution method. Industry practice (Satoshi test, micro-deposit, signed message, KYT-vendor probabilistic attribution) is acceptable provided the method is documented in the AML manual, applied consistently, and produces an audit trail. Unhosted-wallet transfers are not exempted — they trigger risk-based EDD with documented rationale even if all other CDD factors are low-risk.

Politically exposed persons

PEP regime — always EDD, always senior approval

PEP status forces the relationship into EDD regardless of amount or transactional profile. Coverage extends to family up to 2nd degree and close associates. Status persists for 5 years after the public function ceases.

Operational requirements

  • Self-declaration on every CDD form + cross-check against a recognised PEP database (Refinitiv World-Check / Dow Jones / ComplyAdvantage / similar)
  • Senior-management written approval to establish or continue the relationship Instr. Art. 13 (Reform 2)

Sanctions screening

Three lists, three moments, three actions

Three lists are statutorily binding. OFAC / EU / UK are commercial-risk only. Screen at three moments: onboarding, every transaction, and on every list update.

Freeze procedure on UNSC / national hit

Steps 1–3 · Immediate
  1. Confirm the match (not a false positive after basic verification). Record source, timestamp, evidence.
  2. Hold all transactions involving the matched person — funds, wallets, pending operations. Do not execute until FGR (Office of the Attorney General) issues instructions. LECAT Art. 37
  3. Report to FGR via UIF (goAML) without any delay. FGR has up to 24 hours to respond with the formal block / unblock order; the freeze itself is ordered by FGR.
Steps 4–5 · Discipline
  1. No tipping-off — do not explain the hold; do not return funds without UIF / judicial authorisation. D-426 Art. 44
  2. Cooperate with UIF / FGR information requests; retain full file 15 years.
If the match is on a related party (not the direct client), apply same procedure plus immediate refresh of the BO chain to determine actual exposure.

Restricted jurisdictions list. Maintain an internal Restricted Jurisdictions list as part of the Risk Management Policy. The model used by the Decision Wizard combines: (i) FATF Call for Action (DPRK, Iran, Myanmar) and UNSC-sanctioned states → geofence + reject; (ii) OFAC-comprehensive countries (Cuba, Crimea / DNR / LNR Ukraine) → geofence + reject; (iii) crypto-banned countries (China, Bangladesh, Egypt, Morocco, Algeria, Nepal, Oman, Qatar, Tunisia) → geofence + reject; (iv) FATF Grey List + partial-sanctions countries → EDD + senior approval.

Unusual & suspicious transactions

Distinguish unusual from suspicious — the file shows the difference

Unusual operations are deviations from profile or market practice — they trigger analysis, not necessarily a report. Suspicious operations involve reasonable indicia of laundering, terrorism financing or weapons-proliferation financing — they trigger the STR pipeline. The CO is the gatekeeper; the file shows the path.

Unusual operation (operación inusual)

Deviates from the customer's declared profile or normal market practice but does not yet rise to suspicion.

Action: open analysis ticket, request explanation, refresh CDD. Document outcome — close with rationale or escalate to suspicious.

Analyst level → CO informed via daily / weekly ticket roll-up. Instr. Arts. 36, 41, 42
Suspicious operation (operación sospechosa)

Reasonable indicia that funds may relate to laundering, terrorism financing, weapons-proliferation financing, or any predicate offence — irrespective of amount.

Action: CO determination triggers the 24-h STR pipeline (next section). Tipping-off prohibited at every stage.

CO determines; clock starts at signed determination. D-426 Art. 24

Tipping-off — operational red line. No disclosure to the client, counterparty, family, third party, or media about: existence of analysis · filing or non-filing of an STR · UIF requests · freezes · investigation · the AML reason for any account closure. Decline or delay with a neutral ground ("documentation pending") and never mention analysis or reporting. D-426 Art. 44

Suspicious transaction reports

From red flag to STR — the 5-stage flow

Every STR follows the same five stages. Internal analysis runs up to 15 working days, extendable once by request. The filing window is 24 hours from the CO's signed determination — wall-clock, including weekends.

1
Alert opened
KYT alert / staff escalation / pattern detected. Timestamped record created in the CO queue. Source, evidence and trigger logged immediately.
2
Internal analysis — ≤ 15 working days D-426 Art. 24
CO reviews facts, compares with profile, runs KYT and blockchain trace, checks counterparties, documents conclusions. Extension allowed once for the same period upon prior request to UIF.
3
Determination — signed and timestamped
CO records: "Suspicion confirmed [or not] — at [ISO timestamp] — by [CO name]". This is the legal trigger for the 24-h clock. Without a recorded timestamp the clock is ambiguous and UIF reads it against the entity.
4
STR filed — 24 hours wall-clock D-426 Art. 24
File via goAML with the CDD file, Travel Rule data set, KYT report, supporting documents. Report must be timely, complete and concrete.
5
Post-filing
Relationship decision (enhanced monitoring or neutral-ground discontinuation — never cite STR as reason). Tipping-off prohibited. Cooperate with UIF / FGR information requests. Retain full file 15 years.

Attempted operations. If a client attempts but does not consummate a suspicious operation, file an attempted-operation STR through the same pipeline. The Instructivo gives 5 working days from determination of suspicion Instr. Art. 47; the D-426 24-h rule remains the conservative posture (see conflict callout below).

Conflict of norms — STR filing deadline. D-426 Art. 24 sets a 24-hour filing window from determination. Instr. Art. 43 gives 5 working days for the same step. The statute (D-426) prevails by hierarchy. Operational posture: file within 24 h, log the determination timestamp signed by the CO, and treat the Instructivo's 5-wd as a maximum outer envelope only. Same conflict appears for attempted operations (Instr. Art. 47 = 5 wd).

STR insulation — no liability for the Reporting Entity. The STR is not considered a criminal complaint, requires no formalities of such a proceeding, and entails no criminal or civil liability against the Reporting Entity, its representatives, or employees. Instr. Art. 44

STR confidentiality and use

  • The STR is confidential, has no probative value, is used exclusively for intelligence purposes, and is not incorporated into administrative or judicial files D-426 Art. 24
  • The amount of the operation is irrelevant for the purposes of STR D-426 Art. 24
  • STR + voluntary STR + STR for press-news-derived information all go through the same channel Instr. Arts. 48, 49, 50

Red flags catalogue

EDD triggers — any single flag activates EDD

Any single red flag below forces EDD regardless of amount. Use this catalogue at onboarding, relationship review, and every KYT-alert triage. Instr. Art. 13 (Reform 2)

Structuring patterns

Split transactions, round-numbers just under thresholds ($1,000 / $10,000 / $25,000), multiple same-day operations from same source.

→ EDD + STR consideration
Profile deviation

Amount, frequency, asset choice, geography inconsistent with declared activity / occupation / expected volume.

→ Profile refresh + EDD + documented rationale
Cash-intensive counterpart

Money-service business, cash retail, import-export with cash legs, high-cash flow.

→ EDD + documentary SoF + enhanced monitoring
Opaque ownership

Nominees, bearer shares, layered holdings, opaque trust, mailbox address, unexplained BO change.

→ Resolve to natural person mandatory
Flagged wallet

Counterparty wallet linked to mixer, darknet marketplace, exploit, sanctions-linked address, high-risk exchange.

→ EDD + KYT trace retained + STR consideration
Third-party funder

Payment from a party other than the client; "someone else pays for me".

→ Identify the third party; treat as parallel client or reject
Rapid in-out

Funds deposited and transferred out within a short window with no economic rationale (pass-through pattern).

→ EDD + block pending explanation
Document inconsistency

Name / date / signature discrepancy across ID, utility bill, bank statement, corporate certificate.

→ Re-verify via independent source; refusal → decline + STR
High-risk jurisdiction

FATF Grey / Black list, non-cooperating territory, sanctioned state, jurisdiction in UIF / CNAD high-risk advisory.

→ EDD baseline for any counterparty touching the jurisdiction
PEP involvement

Client is a PEP (self / family ≤2° / close associate) or becomes one during the relationship.

→ EDD + SoF + SoW + senior approval; status persists 5 yr post-cease
Adverse media

Credible press / law-enforcement / regulator reference to the client or related party — investigation, sanctions mention, indictment, arrest.

→ Documented review + EDD + STR consideration
Client refusal

Client declines CDD data, BO update, SoF documents, or asks to skip Travel Rule fields.

→ Onboarding prohibited; attempted operation reportable Instr. Art. 47

goAML. The UIF receives reports via the goAML electronic platform (UNODC standard, deployed widely across LATAM FIUs). Each obligated subject is enrolled with credentials issued at registration. STR templates, threshold reports and freeze notifications all submit through goAML. Keep CO and Alternate credentials current and update UIF on any role change.

Two clocks — never confuse them. The STR clock is wall-clock 24 hours from a CO's signed determination — it does not stop on weekends. The Operación Regulada clock (and most other deadlines) is business-day. Missing either is a standalone administrative offence.

Where each report lands — recipients map

The Reporting Hub above lists every recurring or event-driven report. Below is the underlying recipient map, because the four authorities have non-overlapping roles and the routing matters when filing.

UIF (Financial Intelligence Unit)

Receives all operational AML reports via goAML — from both DASPs and BSPs through the same pipeline:

  • STR (24 h) and Attempted-Operation STR D-426 Art. 24
  • Operación Regulada — cash > $10K / other means > $25K (5 wd) Instr. Art. 51
  • Monthly Art. 52 reports — international / local e-transfers ≥ $1,000, family remittances ≥ $200
  • Sanctions / UNSC freeze reports (immediate, then FGR formally orders the freeze) LECAT Art. 37
  • CO / Alternate appointment + removal notices
  • BO / registration-data updates Instr. Art. 23
CNAD (Digital Assets Commission)

Receives DASP-specific licensing and supervisory reports:

  • Quarterly — Digital Asset Issuances RPSAD Art. 15
  • Quarterly — Stablecoins Admitted RPSAD Art. 16
  • Quarterly — Pricing & Commissions LEAD Art. 21(m)
  • Material registration change (5 bd) RPSAD Art. 9
  • Cybersecurity incident (without delay) RPSAD Art. 26
  • Annual cybersecurity test results
BCR (Central Reserve Bank) — Bitcoin track only

BCR's role is Registry-administration for the Bitcoin Service Provider track. No ongoing operational AML reports flow to BCR. For DASPs that also operate with Bitcoin (per Instr. Art. 84-A Reform 1), the BCR-facing flow is:

  • BSP registration application (one-time) — Bitcoin Law Regulations D-27/2021 Art. 3
  • Pre-operations documentation — within 20 bd before going live (deed of incorporation + IDs of partners) D-27/2021 Art. 4
  • Registry update notifications — material changes to registered data (new services, new public-wallet addresses, custodian changes) so that the Registry stays current; BCR removes from the Registry within 72 hours upon discontinuation D-27/2021 Art. 3
  • Discontinuation notice — immediate, on cessation of BSP status
  • Open clause — any other requirement the BCR (or SSF) may issue ad hoc D-27/2021 Art. 4 final
  • Ongoing AML supervision is delegated to SSF (per D-27/2021 Art. 5), not to BCR
NRP-29 (CNBCR-12/2021) and NRP-36 are BCR Technical Rules for banks and EMIs — Art. 1 NRP-29 explicitly excludes BSPs. They matter only if the DASP's banking partner handles Bitcoin flows.
FGR (Office of the Attorney General)

Receives sanctions-freeze reports via UIF (goAML routes them through). FGR is the body that formally orders the freeze on UNSC / national-list matches, within 24 hours of the entity's notification.

  • Sanctions / UNSC list match — entity reports to FGR via UIF; FGR orders the freeze LECAT Art. 37
  • Criminal referrals from STR follow-up

Records & retention

15 years — every AML record, accessible to UIF

All AML records — CDD files, transactions, analyses, STRs, sanctions hits, training, audits, committee minutes — retained 15 years from the operation date or from the termination of the commercial relationship. Format: physical, digital, or electronic.

What to retain
  • CDD file per client: ID, address, occupation, BO chart, SoF, PEP screening, risk scoring
  • Travel Rule records: data per transfer, batch files, transmission logs
  • KYT logs: blockchain traces, alerts, dispositions
  • Sanctions screening: every hit, match record, decision rationale
  • STR analysis files: alert through determination through filing
  • Reports filed: STR, Operación Regulada, monthly Art. 52, freeze reports
  • Training records: attendance, evaluation, dated materials, CO sign-off
  • Audits: internal + external AML, cyber, financial
  • Committee minutes: Prevention Committee + Board AML
How to retain
  • Format: physical, digital or electronic — equally valid
  • Organisation: per client; per transaction within client; chronological within type
  • Accessibility: retrievable on UIF / CNAD request without delay
  • Integrity: tamper-evident storage; version-control on policy documents
  • Backups: secure, geo-redundant, encryption at rest
  • Access control: role-based, audit-logged, principle of least privilege
  • Disposal: only after the 15-year period expires AND no live investigation; documented destruction
Wind-down: on relationship end, retention clock starts from last operation. On corporate dissolution, records remain subject to the 15-year rule via custodian. D-426 Art. 26

Frequently asked

Compliance FAQ

Is the $1,000 CDD trigger for DASPs binding?
Yes. D-426 Art. 15(b) anchors the CDD trigger to the applicable reporting threshold for the relevant transaction type. For e-transfers the reporting threshold is $1,000 per Instructivo Art. 52(a)(b). Therefore CDD is statutorily required for any occasional e-transfer ≥ $1,000. Internal AML policy may calibrate a lower threshold per FATF R.10 — that is internal practice, not statute.
Why does the Travel Rule apply at any amount but Standard CDD only above $1,000?
Two different obligations. Travel Rule (Instructivo Art. 52 par. 2 reformed) requires originator + beneficiary fields on every electronic transfer regardless of amount — the Spanish CAPS text explicitly says "even those that do not exceed the established thresholds". Standard CDD (D-426 Art. 15(b)) is triggered when the amount crosses the relevant reporting threshold. Below $1,000 with no risk factor: Simplified DD applies (Instr. Art. 14 / D-426 Art. 17) — identification with deferred verification, reduced monitoring — plus Travel Rule data + sanctions screen on every transfer regardless.
Where is the Travel Rule statutory anchor for VASPs?
Two anchors layered together: (i) Instr. Art. 84-B(1)(e) — Reform 1 establishes that VASPs must "maintain customer and operation records that allow knowledge of the origin and destination of virtual-asset transactions"; (ii) Instr. Art. 52 par. 2 — Reform 2 sets out the actual data set and the no-threshold rule (financial institutions guarantee that all electronic transfers are accompanied by the required information, even those not exceeding the established thresholds).
What is the difference between an STR clock (24h) and an Operación Regulada clock (5 wd)?
Different triggers, different clocks. STR fires on a CO determination of suspicion — 24 hours wall-clock from the signed determination, including weekends, per D-426 Art. 24. Operación Regulada fires on amount thresholds (cash > $10K or other means > $25K) — 5 working days. Both can co-exist on the same transaction (large + suspicious): file both. Note: Instructivo Art. 43 says 5 wd for STR — that conflicts with D-426 24h. The statute prevails by hierarchy; conservative posture is to file within 24 h.
Where is tipping-off prohibited — LECAT or D-426?
D-426 Art. 44 ("Improper Disclosure of Information") is the tipping-off provision. LECAT Art. 17 in the current text is about port and maritime security offences, not tipping-off. Internal AML manuals citing "LECAT Art. 17 tipping-off" are using outdated mapping — update to D-426 Art. 44.
Does OFAC sanctions screening apply to a Salvadoran DASP?
Not as Salvadoran AML statute — only UNSC and UIF national lists are binding under LECAT and the Instructivo. OFAC matters through (i) banking-partner contracts, (ii) USD-corridor exposure routed via US correspondents, (iii) general commercial-risk management. Manage OFAC in commercial risk policy, not the AML body. EU and UK sanctions are similarly out-of-scope for Salvadoran AML statute but matter under contracts and risk policy.
Can a DASP serve a client from a country on the EDD jurisdictions list?
Yes, with EDD baseline + senior-management written approval. The Restricted Jurisdictions list places FATF Grey List + partial-sanctions countries in the EDD-eligible category. Apply enhanced monitoring, KYT on every transaction, full SoF/SoW, periodic re-screening. If risk appetite does not allow → decline with neutral ground (never cite jurisdiction).
Who signs the determination?
The Compliance Officer (or Alternate, if delegated). Determination must be timestamped (ISO format) and signed (digital signature acceptable). Without a signed timestamp the 24-h clock is ambiguous and UIF reads it against the entity. Default operational discipline: file same business day as determination.
How often must sanctions and PEP lists be refreshed?
Quarterly minimum + on every list update + per-transaction at execution. UNSC publishes updates regularly; subscribe to the official feeds. PEP databases (Refinitiv / Dow Jones / ComplyAdvantage / similar) update continuously — schedule daily re-screen against the active client base.
What records survive a DASP wind-down?
All AML records — 15 years from the operation date or from the termination of the commercial relationship, regardless of corporate dissolution (D-426 Art. 26). The wind-down plan must transfer or escrow records to a designated custodian. Client assets in custody: returned or transferred to another licensed DASP. Records remain accessible to UIF on request through the entire 15-year period.

Glossary

Acronyms & key terms

AML / CTF
Anti-Money Laundering / Counter-Terrorism Financing.
BO / UBO
Beneficial Owner / Ultimate Beneficial Owner — the natural person who ultimately owns or controls the client. Statutory thresholds: ≥10% (Instr. Arts. 12, 20 — apply as floor); ≥25% (D-426 Art. 15); majority shareholding / control by other means (Instr. Art. 21-A Reform 2). Trusts: identify all parties (Instr. Art. 21).
CDD
Customer Due Diligence — measures in Instr. Art. 12. Triggered by ongoing relationship (D-426 Art. 15(a)) or occasional transaction above the applicable reporting threshold (D-426 Art. 15(b) → Instr. Arts. 51, 52). For DASP e-transfers: $1,000 statutory floor.
CO / Alternate CO
Compliance Officer / Alternate Compliance Officer. AML/CFT-certified, UIF-registered. No residency requirement for DASP COs (Instr. Art. 84-B Reform 1).
CNAD
Comisión Nacional de Activos Digitales — Salvadoran DASP supervisory authority. Issues PSAD registration; enforces RPSAD; receives DASP-specific filings.
D-426
Decree 426 — Special Law for the Prevention, Control and Punishment of Asset Laundering, Financing of Terrorism, and Financing of the Proliferation of Weapons of Mass Destruction. The current AML Law.
D-108
Decree 108 — Special Law against Acts of Terrorism (LECAT). Source of the UNSC sanctions freeze obligation (Art. 37).
DASP / PSAD
Digital Asset Service Provider / Proveedor de Servicios de Activos Digitales. Licensed by CNAD under LEAD. In the Instructivo, called "Provider of Virtual Asset Services" (Arts. 84-A/84-B/84-C Reform 1).
EDD
Enhanced Due Diligence — Instr. Art. 13 (Reform 2) measures. Triggered by any risk factor regardless of amount. Requires senior-management written approval.
FATF R.16
FATF Recommendation 16 — wire-transfer / Travel Rule standard. Imported into Salvadoran statute via Instructivo Art. 52 par. 2 reformed and Art. 84-B(1)(e) Reform 1.
goAML
UNODC-developed FIU reporting platform — El Salvador's UIF uses goAML for STR, Operación Regulada, monthly Art. 52 reports, and freeze notifications.
Identity verification
Reform 2 obligation under Instr. Art. 12: customer / user identity must be verified BEFORE the commercial relationship begins, via official identification documents issued by competent public authorities. If not verified, the relationship cannot start.
Instructivo UIF
Operational rulebook from the Financial Investigation Unit. Reform 1 added VASP-specific provisions (Arts. 84-A, 84-B, 84-C); Reform 2 amended CDD (Art. 12), EDD (Art. 13) and Travel Rule (Art. 52 par. 2).
KYT
Know-Your-Transaction — blockchain analytics for transaction monitoring. Tools: Chainalysis, TRM Labs, Elliptic, Crystal, Merkle Science. Operationally required for DASPs.
LECAT
Special Law against Acts of Terrorism (D-108). Source of UNSC sanctions freeze (Art. 37). Tipping-off is in D-426 Art. 44, not in LECAT.
Operación Regulada
Threshold report under D-426 Art. 25 + Instr. Art. 51: cash > $10K or other means > $25K (single op or monthly aggregate per client). Filed within 5 working days. Includes "transactions in digital assets" by name (D-426 Art. 25).
PEP
Politically Exposed Person — domestic / foreign / international-organisation public function. Coverage: self + family ≤ 2nd degree + close associates. Always EDD; status persists 5 yr post-cease (D-426 Art. 19).
SoF / SoW
Source of Funds (transaction-specific) / Source of Wealth (overall patrimony). SoF: declarative at CDD baseline, documentary at EDD. SoW: not required at CDD; documentary at EDD. PEP: both mandatory.
STR
Suspicious Transaction Report (Reporte de Operación Sospechosa). Filed to UIF within 24 hours of CO determination (D-426 Art. 24). Confidential; no probative value; intelligence purposes only; amount irrelevant.
Travel Rule
Per-transfer originator + beneficiary data requirement on every electronic transfer regardless of amount. Source: Instr. Art. 52 par. 2 (Reform 2) + Instr. Art. 84-B(1)(e) (Reform 1). Includes batch-processing rule for grouped transfers.
UIF
Unidad de Investigación Financiera — Salvadoran Financial Intelligence Unit. Receives all AML reports via goAML.
UNSC sanctions
UN Security Council consolidated sanctions lists (1267, 1373, 1718, 1988, 2231 + successors). Binding via LECAT Art. 37.
