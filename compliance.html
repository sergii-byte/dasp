Customer Due Diligence

CDD by trigger — what activates, when

El Salvador anchors the CDD trigger in AML Law Art. 15. Two paths trigger full CDD: (a) establishment of an ongoing relationship, or (b) an occasional transaction above the applicable reporting threshold for that transaction type. The reporting threshold cascades through the Instructivo (Art. 51 cash / Art. 52 e-transfers), so the CDD trigger differs by channel. EDD is a separate layer — triggered by risk factors, not amount.

The four parallel triggers

Occasional e-transfer (intl / local via app or device) ≥ $1,000 Standard CDD AML Law Art. 15(b) → Instr. Art. 52(a)(b)
Occasional family remittance ≥ $200 Standard CDD AML Law Art. 15(b) → Instr. Art. 52(c)
Occasional cash transaction > $10,000 Standard CDD + Operación Regulada report AML Law Art. 15(b) → Instr. Art. 51
Occasional other means (cheque, wire, card not under Art. 52) > $25,000 Standard CDD + Operación Regulada report AML Law Art. 15(b) → Instr. Art. 51

Below all thresholds AND no risk factor — (only Travel Rule) No statutory CDD — collect Travel Rule data + sanctions screen only Instr. Art. 52 par.2 reformed + LECAT Art. 37

DASP-relevant note: most DASP operations are e-transfers — the operative CDD trigger is therefore $1,000 per the Art. 15(b) → Art. 52(a) chain. Internal AML policy may calibrate a lower threshold per FATF R.10 risk-based principles, but $1,000 is the statutory floor.

Beneficial owner — apply 10% as floor

Statutory thresholds

  • ≥ 10% ownership or control Instr. Arts. 12, 20 — the stricter rule, apply as floor
  • ≥ 25% ownership or control D-426 Art. 2
  • Trusts: identify all parties — settlor, trustee, beneficiaries, protector Instr. Art. 21
  • Resolve to natural person through every layer

Refresh triggers — out of cycle

  • Change in ownership ≥10%
  • Change in authorised signatories
  • Trust party change / nominee change
  • BO becomes PEP
  • Sanctions list update naming related party
  • Material deviation from declared profile

Scheduled refresh: Standard CDD every 3 yr · EDD every year · half-yearly if score very high.

Travel Rule

Per-transfer data — Art. 52 par. 2 reformed

Reform (2) of Instructivo Art. 52 imports a Travel Rule into the binding statutory framework. Seven fields are mandatory on every electronic transfer regardless of amount — the Spanish text uses upper case to underscore that this applies even to transfers below the Art. 52(a)(b) reporting thresholds. The institution must ensure transmission to the beneficiary's institution, and a batch-processing rule applies when grouping individual transfers.

The seven mandatory fields

Originator (sender)

  1. Name of the originating customer
  2. Account number of the originator
  3. Type and number of identity document (DUI / NIT / passport)

For natural persons: DUI or passport. For legal entities: NIT or registration number.

Beneficiary (recipient)

  1. Name of the beneficiary
  2. Account number of the beneficiary
  3. Name of the beneficiary's bank (bank administering the account)

If the beneficiary VASP cannot supply the bank name (unhosted wallet), document the limitation and apply risk-based EDD.

Transaction

  1. Transaction identifier (transaction reference / hash / unique ID)

Source: Instr. Art. 52 par.2 reformed. Spanish text in CAPS: "LAS INSTITUCIONES FINANCIERAS DEBEN GARANTIZAR QUE TODAS LAS TRANSFERENCIAS ELECTRÓNICAS … INCLUSO AQUELLAS QUE NO SUPEREN LOS UMBRALES ESTABLECIDOS, SEAN ACOMPAÑADAS MÍNIMAMENTE DE LA INFORMACIÓN REQUERIDA".

Batch-processing rule. When several individual electronic transfers from a single originator are grouped into one batch file for transmission to multiple beneficiaries, the file must contain the required and accurate originator information and the complete beneficiary information for each transfer, under the systems the UIF determines. Instr. Art. 52 par.2 final

Operational obligations by VASP role

A DASP can occupy three roles on the same transfer chain — sometimes simultaneously when intermediating. Each role triggers its own duties at the moment the transfer enters the order book.

Originating DASP

  1. Verify originator (CDD if triggered, sanctions screen always)
  2. Attribute originating wallet to verified client
  3. Collect 7 Travel Rule fields
  4. Screen beneficiary against UNSC + UIF national
  5. Transmit data set securely to beneficiary VASP
  6. Retain full record 15 years

Beneficiary DASP

  1. Verify beneficiary (CDD if triggered)
  2. Attribute beneficiary wallet to verified client
  3. Receive + validate 7 Travel Rule fields
  4. Flag missing fields; hold pending data
  5. Screen originator against UNSC + UIF national
  6. Retain + monitor; STR if pattern emerges

Intermediary DASP

  1. Pass 7 Travel Rule fields unchanged forward
  2. Validate completeness on receipt
  3. Refuse to forward if fields missing
  4. Screen both originator and beneficiary
  5. Apply transaction monitoring at routing point
  6. Retain 15 years even with no client relationship

Wallet attribution is the operational pinch point. Art. 52 par.2 requires originator/beneficiary identity but does not specify a wallet-attribution method. Industry practice (Satoshi test, micro-deposit, signed message, KYT-vendor probabilistic attribution) is acceptable provided the method is documented in the AML manual, applied consistently, and produces an audit trail. Unhosted-wallet transfers are not exempted — they trigger risk-based EDD with documented rationale.

goAML. El Salvador's UIF receives reports via the goAML electronic platform (UNODC standard, deployed widely across LATAM FIUs). Each obligated subject is enrolled with credentials issued at registration. STR templates, threshold reports and freeze notifications all submit through goAML. CO and Deputy CO are the registered submitters — keep their credentials current and update UIF within the statutory windows on any role change.

Two clocks, never confuse them. The STR clock is wall-clock 24 hours from a CO's signed determination of suspicion — it does not stop on weekends. The Operación Regulada clock (and most other deadlines) is business-day. Missing either is a standalone administrative offence; supervisors audit dates before substance.

Suspicious Transaction Reports

From red flag to STR — the 5-stage flow

Every STR follows the same five stages. The internal analysis runs up to 15 business days; the filing window is 24 hours from the CO's signed determination. No tipping-off at any stage.

Stage 1 · Alert opened

KYT alert / staff escalation / pattern detected. Timestamped record created in the CO queue. Source, evidence, and trigger are logged immediately.

Stage 2 · Internal analysis

≤ 15 business days D-426 Art. 24. CO reviews facts, compares with profile, runs KYT and blockchain trace, checks counterparties, documents conclusions. Extension allowed once with documented reason.

Stage 3 · Determination

CO records a signed, timestamped determination line: "Suspicion confirmed [or not] — at [ISO timestamp] — by [CO name]". This is the legal trigger for the 24-h clock. Without a recorded timestamp, the clock is ambiguous and UIF reads it against the entity.

Stage 4 · STR filed

24 hours wall-clock from determination D-426 Art. 24. File via goAML / UIF platform with full Travel Rule data, KYT report, supporting docs.

Stage 5 · Post-filing

  • Relationship decision: enhanced monitoring, neutral-ground discontinuation — never cite STR as reason
  • Tipping-off prohibited — no disclosure to client, family, third party, or media LECAT Art. 17
  • Cooperate with UIF / FGR information requests
  • Retain full file 15 years D-426 Art. 26

Suspicious vs. unusual — distinguish in the file

Unusual operation (operación inusual)

Deviates from the client's declared profile or normal market practice but does not yet rise to suspicion. Triggers analysis — not necessarily an STR. Document the unusual flag, request explanation, refresh CDD, escalate if pattern persists.

Analyst level; CO informed via daily/weekly ticket roll-up.

Suspicious operation (operación sospechosa)

Reasonable indicia that funds may relate to laundering, terrorism financing, weapons proliferation, or any predicate offence — irrespective of amount. Triggers STR pipeline (5 stages above).

CO determination required; 24-h filing clock starts at signed determination.

Tipping-off — operational red line. No disclosure to the client (or counterparty, family, third party) about: existence of analysis · filing or non-filing of STR · UIF requests · freezes · investigation · the AML reason for any account closure. Decline or delay with a neutral ground ("documentation pending") and never mention analysis or reporting. LECAT Art. 17

Sanctions Screening

Three lists, three moments, three actions

Three lists are statutorily binding; OFAC/EU/UK are commercial-risk only. Screen at three moments: onboarding, every transaction, periodic sweep on list updates.

Freeze procedure on UNSC / national hit

Steps 1–3 · Immediate

  1. Confirm match (not false positive after basic verification). Record source, timestamp, evidence.
  2. Freeze immediately — funds, wallets, pending operations.
  3. Report UIF within 3 business days via goAML with match record + frozen amount LECAT Art. 37

Steps 4–5 · Discipline

  1. No tipping-off — do not explain the hold, do not return funds without UIF / judicial authorisation LECAT Art. 17
  2. Cooperate with UIF / FGR information requests; retain full file 15 years

If the match is on a related party (not the direct client) — same procedure, plus immediate refresh of the BO chain to determine actual exposure.

Restricted jurisdictions list — internal. Each DASP maintains its own Restricted Jurisdictions list as part of its Risk Management Policy. The model used by the Wizard below combines: (i) FATF Call for Action (NK, Iran, Myanmar) and UNSC-sanctioned states → geofence + reject; (ii) OFAC-comprehensive countries (Cuba, Crimea/DNR/LNR Ukraine) → geofence + reject; (iii) Crypto-banned countries (China, Bangladesh, etc.) → geofence + reject; (iv) FATF Grey List + partial-sanctions countries → EDD + senior approval if accepted.

Politically Exposed Persons

PEP regime — always EDD, always senior approval

PEP status forces the relationship into EDD regardless of amount or transaction profile. PEP screening covers self, family up to 2nd degree, and close associates. Status persists 5 years post-cease of public function.

Red Flags & Unusual Transactions

EDD triggers — any single flag activates EDD

Any single red flag below forces EDD regardless of amount Instr. Art. 13. Use this catalogue at onboarding, relationship review, and every KYT-alert triage.

Structuring patterns

Split transactions, round-numbers just under thresholds ($1,000 / $10,000 / $25,000), multiple same-day operations from same source. → EDD + STR consideration.

Profile deviation

Amount, frequency, asset choice, geography inconsistent with declared activity / occupation / expected volume. → Profile refresh + EDD + documented rationale.

Cash-intensive counterpart

Money-service business, cash retail, import-export with cash legs, high-cash flow. → EDD + documentary SoF + enhanced monitoring.

Opaque ownership

Nominees, bearer shares, layered holdings, opaque trust, mailbox address, unexplained BO change. → Resolve to natural person mandatory Instr. Arts. 20, 21.

Flagged wallet

Counterparty wallet linked to mixer, darknet marketplace, exploit, sanctions-linked address, high-risk exchange. → EDD + KYT trace retained + STR consideration.

Third-party funder

Payment from a party other than the client; "someone else pays for me". → Identify the third party; treat as parallel client or reject Instr. Art. 12(e).

Rapid in-out

Funds deposited and transferred out within a short window with no economic rationale (pass-through). → EDD + block pending explanation.

Document inconsistency

Name / date / signature discrepancy across ID, utility bill, bank statement, corporate certificate. → Re-verify via independent source; refusal → decline + STR.

High-risk jurisdiction

FATF Grey/Black list, non-cooperating territory, sanctioned state, jurisdiction in UIF / CNAD high-risk advisory. → EDD baseline for any counterparty touching the jurisdiction.

PEP involvement

Client is a PEP (self / family ≤ 2° / close associate) or becomes one during relationship. → EDD + SoF + SoW + senior-mgmt approval Instr. Art. 14. PEP status persists 5 years post-cease.

Adverse media

Credible press / law-enforcement / regulator reference to client or related party — investigation, sanctions mention, indictment, arrest. → Documented review + EDD + STR consideration.

Client refusal

Client declines to provide CDD data, update BO, produce SoF documents, or asks you to skip Travel Rule fields. → Onboarding prohibited Instr. Art. 12; attempted operation reportable Instr. Art. 47.

Unusual ≠ Suspicious — handle differently

Unusual = analyse and document. A profile deviation triggers an unusual flag. Open a ticket, request client explanation, refresh CDD if needed. Document outcome — close with rationale or escalate to suspicious.

Suspicious = STR pipeline. Once the CO determines suspicion, the 24-h filing clock starts. Tipping-off prohibited. Relationship decision separate from filing decision.

Records & Retention

15 years — every AML record, accessible to UIF

All AML records — CDD files, transactions, analyses, STRs, sanctions hits, training, audits, committee minutes — retained 15 years from relationship end or operation date. Format: physical, digital or electronic, organised per client.

What to retain

  • CDD file per client: ID, address, occupation, BO chart, SoF, PEP screening, risk scoring
  • Travel Rule records: 7 fields per transfer, batch files, transmission logs
  • KYT logs: blockchain traces, alerts, dispositions
  • Sanctions screening: every hit, match record, decision rationale
  • STR analysis files: alert through determination through filing
  • Reports filed: ROS, Operación Regulada, monthly Art. 52, freeze reports
  • Training records: attendance, evaluation, dated materials, CO sign-off
  • Audits: internal + external AML, cyber, financial
  • Committee minutes: Prevention Committee quarterly + Board AML

How to retain

  • Format: physical, digital, or electronic — equally valid Instr. Art. 12(d)
  • Organisation: per client; per transaction within client; chronological within type
  • Accessibility: must be retrievable on UIF / CNAD request without delay
  • Integrity: tamper-evident storage; version-control on policy documents
  • Backups: secure, geo-redundant, encryption at rest
  • Access control: role-based, audit-logged, principle of least privilege
  • Disposal: only after the 15-year period expires AND no live investigation; documented destruction

Wind-down: on relationship end, retention clock starts from last operation. On DASP wind-down, records remain subject to the 15-year rule independent of corporate dissolution D-426 Art. 26.

Frequently Asked

Compliance FAQ

Is the $1,000 CDD trigger for DASPs binding?
Yes. AML Law Art. 15(b) anchors the CDD trigger to the applicable reporting threshold for the relevant transaction type. For e-transfers, the reporting threshold is $1,000 per Instructivo Art. 52(a)(b). Therefore CDD is statutorily required for any occasional e-transfer ≥ $1,000. (Internal AML policy may calibrate a lower threshold per FATF R.10 — that is internal practice, not statute.)
Why does the Travel Rule apply at any amount but CDD only above $1,000?
Two different obligations. Travel Rule (Instructivo Art. 52 par. 2 reformed) requires 7 originator + beneficiary fields on every electronic transfer regardless of amount — the Spanish CAPS text explicitly says "even those that do not exceed the established thresholds". CDD (AML Law Art. 15(b)) is triggered only when the amount crosses the relevant reporting threshold ($1,000 for e-transfers). Below $1,000 with no risk factor: collect Travel Rule data + sanctions screen, that's it.
Where does the 8-element Travel Rule list come from?
It came from the CNAD AML/CFT Risk Management Guide for the Digital Assets Industry (2023), §2(b), which cited the now-restructured Instructivo Art. 84-B(1)(e). Reform (2) of the Instructivo relocated the binding Travel Rule into Art. 52 par. 2 with a different (7-field) data set. This portal cites only Art. 52 par. 2 — the current statutory source. See the Travel Rule section.
What is the difference between an STR clock (24h) and an Operación Regulada clock (5 bd)?
Different triggers, different clocks. STR fires on a CO determination of suspicion — 24 h wall-clock from determination, including weekends. Operación Regulada fires on amount threshold (cash > $10K or other means > $25K) — 5 business days. They can co-exist on the same transaction (large + suspicious): file both.
Does OFAC sanctions screening apply to a Salvadoran DASP?
Not as Salvadoran AML statute — only UNSC and UIF national lists are binding under LECAT and the Instructivo. OFAC matters through (i) banking-partner contracts, (ii) USD-corridor exposure routed via US correspondents, (iii) general commercial-risk management. Manage OFAC in commercial risk policy, not in AML body. EU and UK sanctions are similarly out-of-scope for Salvadoran AML statute but matter under contracts and risk policy.
Can a DASP serve a client from a country on the "EDD" jurisdictions list?
Yes, but with EDD baseline + senior-management written approval. The Restricted Jurisdictions list (Risk Management Policy v.16.04.2026) places FATF Grey List + partial-sanctions countries in Categories 5–6 → EDD-eligible. Apply enhanced monitoring, KYT on every transaction, full SoF/SoW, periodic re-screening. If risk appetite does not allow → decline with neutral ground (never cite jurisdiction).
What goes into an STR file?
The full client CDD file, the Travel Rule data set for the transaction, the KYT report (blockchain trace if applicable), the dated determination line, the analysis memo with facts, profile comparison, conclusion — and any supporting documents (correspondence, bank statements, etc.). File via goAML / UIF platform within 24 hours. Retain the full file 15 years.
Who signs the determination?
The Compliance Officer (or Deputy CO if delegated). Determination must be timestamped (ISO format) and signed (digital signature acceptable). Without a signed timestamp, the 24-h clock is ambiguous and UIF reads it against the entity. Default operational discipline: file same business day as determination.
How often must sanctions and PEP lists be refreshed?
Quarterly minimum + on every list update + per-transaction at execution. UNSC publishes updates regularly; subscribe to the official feeds. PEP databases (Refinitiv / Dow Jones / ComplyAdvantage / similar) update continuously — schedule daily re-screen against the active client base.
What records survive a DASP wind-down?
All AML records — 15 years from operation date, regardless of corporate dissolution D-426 Art. 26. Wind-down plan must transfer or escrow records to a designated custodian. Client assets in custody: returned or transferred to another licensed DASP. Records must remain accessible to UIF on request through the entire 15-year period.

CO / Deputy CO
Compliance Officer / Deputy CO. AML/CFT-certified, UIF-registered. No residency requirement for DASP COs (per personnel exemption in Instructivo).

D-426
AML reform decree (2025) extending the obligated-subject perimeter to DASPs, setting 24-h STR clock (Art. 24), 15-yr retention (Art. 26), 25% BO threshold (Art. 2).
DASP / PSAD
Digital Asset Service Provider / Proveedor de Servicios de Activos Digitales. Licensed by CNAD under LEAD.
EDD
Enhanced Due Diligence — Instr. Art. 13 measures. Triggered by any risk factor regardless of amount: PEP, high-risk jurisdiction, opaque ownership, cash-intensive, structuring. Requires senior-management written approval.
FATF R.16
FATF Recommendation 16 — wire-transfer / Travel Rule standard. Imported into Salvadoran statute via Instructivo Art. 52 par. 2 reformed.
goAML
UNODC-developed FIU reporting platform — El Salvador's UIF uses goAML for STR, Operación Regulada, Art. 52 monthly reports, sanctions freeze notifications.
Instructivo UIF
UIF Instructivo (operational rulebook). Reformed (2) version is current; the older 2021 numbering (incl. Art. 84-B) has been restructured — Travel Rule is now Art. 52 par. 2.
KYT
Know-Your-Transaction — blockchain analytics for transaction monitoring. Tools: Chainalysis, TRM Labs, Elliptic, Crystal, Merkle Science. Operationally required for DASPs; not statutorily named.
LECAT
Ley Especial contra Actos de Terrorismo — counter-terrorism statute. Art. 17 = tipping-off prohibition; Art. 37 = UNSC sanctions freeze obligation.
Operación Regulada
Threshold report under Instr. Art. 51: cash > $10K or other means > $25K (single op or monthly aggregate per client). Filed within 5 bd.
PEP
Politically Exposed Person — domestic / foreign / international-organisation public function. Coverage: self + family ≤ 2° + close associates. Always EDD; status persists 5 yr post-cease.
SoF / SoW
Source of Funds (transaction-specific) / Source of Wealth (overall patrimony). SoF: declarative at CDD baseline, documentary at EDD. SoW: not required at CDD; documentary at EDD. PEP: both mandatory.
STR / ROS
