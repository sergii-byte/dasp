DASP Registration Flow

Two-phase process per CNAD official procedure. Each interaction cycle: CNAD has 20 business days to respond RPSAD Art. 8, applicant has 10 business days to cure RPSAD Art. 8.

Entity Formation & Personnel Onboarding

Incorporate S.A. de C.V., appoint COs and CISO, register with UIF. Done in parallel.

4–6 wk

Definitive Registration

Application Evaluation

CNAD reviews within 20 bd. If incomplete — 10 bd to cure. Expect 2–3 rounds.

20 bd per round
▾
  • CNAD review: 20 business days RPSAD Art. 8 LEAD Art. 20
  • If incomplete: applicant has 10 business days to cure RPSAD Art. 8
  • Each significant change: +5 business days RPSAD Art. 9
  • CNAD may extend any term by up to 50% RPSAD Art. 44
  • Technical presentation during review: platform, DLT, security, KYC CNAD Procedure
  • Final meeting: CNAD verifies CDD, Travel Rule, PEPs, UBO, training CNAD Guide §6.b

Timeline math: 1 round = 20 bd (CNAD) + 10 bd (applicant) ≈ 6 calendar weeks. With 2–3 rounds: 12–18 weeks.

Definitive Registration

Favorable / Unfavorable Resolution

CNAD issues final decision. Unfavorable = may resubmit. Favorable = proceed to payment.

▾
  • Favorable: proceed to payment within 10 bd RPSAD Art. 10
  • Unfavorable: may resubmit request LEAD Art. 20
  • Registration fee: $6,132 (15 × $408.80) LEAD Art. 12(1)(a)
  • If not paid in time → not considered registered RPSAD Art. 10

Definitive Registration

UIF Notification

CNAD notifies the Financial Investigation Unit of the new DASP registration.

▾
  • Commission notifies UIF of new registered DASP CNAD Procedure
  • DASP now subject to ongoing UIF supervision and reporting obligations

Note on cure periods: LEAD Art. 20 grants 5 business days to cure; RPSAD Art. 8 grants 10 working days. RPSAD is the more recent and specific regulation — in practice, 10 days applies. CNAD may also request additional info before the 20-day deadline expires, specifying a custom term RPSAD Art. 8. Any term can be extended by up to 50% RPSAD Art. 44.

Post-Registration Obligations: Annual renewal $4,088 (10 × $408.80), Q1 each year RPSAD Art. 12. Quarterly reports: first 20 days of Apr/Jul/Oct/Jan RPSAD Arts. 15–16. Non-payment = registration cancelled + tax benefits lost RPSAD Art. 12. Records retention: 15 years AML Law Art. 26.

Scope

DASP Activities Under LEAD

Article 19 defines which activities require DASP registration. Each carries its own AML risk classification.

ActivityLEAD Art.AML Risk CNAD Guide §5.a
Exchange — digital assets for fiat or other digital assets19(a)High
Exchange platform — operate a matching/trading venue19(b)High
Transfer — transfer digital assets for third parties19(f.1)High
Custody & administration — hold/manage digital assets on behalf of clients19(f.2)Above Avg
Order reception/transmission — receive and transmit client orders19(f.3)Above Avg
Order execution — execute orders on behalf of clients19(f.4)Above Avg
Risk assessment & subscriptions — evaluate risks, manage subscriptions19(c)Moderate
Placement — place digital assets on platforms or wallets19(d)Moderate
Investment management — manage digital asset investment products19(e)Moderate

Important: A single DASP can register for multiple activities. The AML risk classification of the highest-risk activity determines the overall compliance burden. Activities not listed in Art. 19 do not require DASP registration.

Legal Framework

Applicable Legislation

The regulatory framework consists of a primary law, DASP-specific regulations, AML legislation, and supervisory guidelines.

LEAD — Ley de Emisión de Activos Digitales

Decreto No. 643, 2023. Primary law governing digital asset service providers, issuers, and certifiers. Establishes CNAD authority, registration requirements, tax benefits, and sanctions framework.

Primary Law

RPSAD — Reglamento para Proveedores de Servicios de Activos Digitales

CNAD-035-2023. Detailed regulation for DASP registration, ongoing obligations, cybersecurity, customer service, and operational rules. 48 articles.

DASP Regulation

AML Law — Ley Contra el Lavado de Dinero y de Activos

Decreto No. 426, 2025. Anti-money laundering and counter-terrorism financing framework. Defines CO obligations, reporting requirements, sanctions.

AML/CFT

UIF Instructivo

Financial Investigation Unit instruction manual. Detailed operational rules for AML compliance: CO qualifications, UIF registration, reporting thresholds, Travel Rule, record-keeping. Art. 84-B specifically addresses DASP requirements.

UIF Guidelines

CNAD AML Guide for DASPs

Practical compliance guide from CNAD. Risk classification per activity, CDD thresholds ($1,000 for occasional), KYT requirements, Travel Rule implementation, final meeting checklist.

Compliance Guide

Issuer & Stablecoin Regulations

CNAD-017-2023 (Issuer Regulation) and Stablecoin Regulation. Govern public/private issuances, RID requirements, certifier process, stablecoin reserve rules. Separate registration track from DASP.

Team Structure

Personnel & Residency Requirements

No nationality requirements for any position. Key selling point: DASP Compliance Officers are exempt from residency.

Director / Legal Representative

  • Board or Sole Administrator RPSAD Art. 7(b)(ii)
  • Credentials in Commercial Registry
  • Foreign: local representative via POA LEAD Art. 18(d)
No residency required

Principal Compliance Officer

  • Board-appointed AML Law Art. 20
  • AML/CFT certification UIF Instr. Art. 64
  • UIF registration within 5 days UIF Instr. Art. 64
  • Independence, highest rank under CEO AML Law Art. 20
No residency for DASPs

Explicit exemption UIF Instr. Art. 84-B(2). Must have employment relationship.

Alternate Compliance Officer

  • Mandatory by law AML Law Art. 20
  • Same qualifications as Principal CO
  • AML/CFT cert + UIF registration UIF Instr. Arts. 3, 64
No residency for DASPs

CISO

  • Board member or manager RPSAD Art. 25(a)
  • Authority, independence, direct Board access RPSAD Art. 25(a)
  • Coordinates cybersecurity plan
No residency required

Prevention Committee AML Law Art. 23: DASPs must form a Prevention Committee — 3+ members including one Board member and CO. Natural persons may opt out of CO designation — they assume all AML duties personally AML Law Art. 22.

Security

Cybersecurity Requirements

RPSAD Arts. 25–26: governance, real-time monitoring, mandatory testing.

Governance Art. 25(a)

Internal procedures, external audits. CISO with authority and independence.

Identification Art. 25(b)

Safeguard mechanisms. Protect order management, custody, acquirer data systems.

Protection Art. 25(c)

SOC, IPS, access controls. Internal threat detection. Employee training.

Detection Art. 25(d)

Continuous real-time monitoring. Multi-level security controls.

Recovery Art. 25(d)(vi)

Critical processes: 6 hours. Transaction settlement: 24 hours.

Testing Art. 26

External auditor. 3 scenarios: confidentiality, availability, integrity.
