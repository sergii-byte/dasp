Track 1

DASP Registration Flow

Digital Asset Service Provider registration is a two-phase process administered by CNAD under LEAD Arts. 18–21 and RPSAD Arts. 5–14.

1

Phase 1 — Pre-Registration

Entity Formation & Incorporation

Establish a Salvadoran legal entity (S.A. de C.V.) or register a branch of a foreign company. Foreign entities must form a corporation or branch domiciled in El Salvador.

▾
Legal Requirements
  • Natural persons: present DUI or resident card, indicate domicile in El Salvador LEAD Art. 18(c)
  • Foreign legal entities: must form a corporation or branch domiciled in El Salvador, registered with the National Registry Center (CNR) LEAD Art. 18(d)
  • Obtain Tax Identification Number (NIT) RPSAD Art. 7(b)(iii)
  • Register Board of Directors or Sole Administrator credentials with Commercial Registry RPSAD Art. 7(b)(ii)
Documents at This Stage
  • Notarized public deed of incorporation, registered with Registry of Commerce RPSAD Art. 7(b)(i)
  • Credential of Board of Directors election or general power of attorney RPSAD Art. 7(b)(ii)
  • Copy of Tax Identification Number card RPSAD Art. 7(b)(iii)
  • Company registration certificate RPSAD Art. 7(d)
2

Phase 1 — Pre-Registration

Mandatory Personnel Onboarding

Appoint Principal and Substitute Compliance Officers and designate a CISO. Register CO and substitute with UIF before submitting the application.

▾
Required Appointments
  • Principal Compliance Officer — appointed by Board of Directors, certified in AML/CFT/WMDP LEAD Art. 21 UIF Instr. Art. 84-B(2)
  • Substitute Compliance Officer — mandatory by law, same qualifications as principal CO AML Law Art. 20
  • CISO / Information Security Officer — Board member or manager with authority and direct access to Board RPSAD Art. 25(a)
CO Qualifications
  • Training in AML/CFT/FPADM prevention UIF Instr. Art. 72
  • Knowledge of applicable regulatory framework UIF Instr. Art. 72
  • Sufficient experience in the economic activity of the entity UIF Instr. Art. 72(2)
  • No residency requirement for DASP Compliance Officers — explicit exemption UIF Instr. Art. 84-B(2)
  • Must maintain employment relationship ("dependencia laboral") with the entity UIF Instr. Art. 84-B(2)
Documents at This Stage
  • Board resolution appointing Principal and Substitute CO AML Law Art. 20
  • AML/CFT/WMDP prevention certifications for both COs UIF Instr. Art. 64
  • UIF platform registration confirmation for both COs UIF Instr. Art. 3
  • Organization chart showing CO position with independence from operational areas RPSAD Art. 7(e)
CO residency exemption for DASPs UIF registration within 5 days of appointment
3

Phase 1 — Pre-Registration

Pre-Registration Application to CNAD

Submit pre-registration form with applicant details and desired operating area. CNAD performs initial evaluation of territorial and material competence.

▾
Process
  • Complete CNAD pre-registration form (electronic) — available at CNAD website CNAD Procedure
  • Provide: applicant details, desired operating area, description of services LEAD Art. 18
  • CNAD reviews for territorial/material competence per LEAD Arts. 2–4 CNAD Procedure
  • CNAD may request additional documentation or clarification
  • CNAD issues "Objection" or "No Objection" — no objection leads to specification of registration requirements CNAD Procedure
4

Phase 1 — Pre-Registration

First Presentation to Regulator

Present the product/service to CNAD. The Commission evaluates the business model, target market, and digital asset services to be offered.

▾
What to Present
  • Business model and digital asset services per LEAD Art. 19 categories
  • List of digital assets with benefits, restrictions, limits, financial/commercial restrictions RPSAD Art. 7(h)
  • General customer service/communication system description RPSAD Art. 7(f)
  • Documentation demonstrating capacity to offer indicated services RPSAD Art. 7(g)
No Objection = proceed to Phase 2 Objection = address concerns, may resubmit
5

Phase 2 — Definitive Registration

Full Documentation Package Submission

Submit the complete document package per LEAD Art. 20 and RPSAD Arts. 7, 17–35. Both digital and physical copies at CNAD offices in San Salvador.

▾
A. Business & Corporate
  • 3-Year Business Plan for El Salvador: executive summary, business description, market strategy, financial projections, budget, KPIs, risks & mitigations, implementation plan CNAD Procedure §1
  • Detailed organizational structure (names, positions, functions) including international structure RPSAD Art. 7(e) LEAD Art. 20(c)
  • Organisation chart and corporate governance document RPSAD Art. 7(e)
B. Customer Service & Communication
  • General explanation of each digital asset: characteristics, interchangeability, price, 12-month history, trading volume RPSAD Art. 22
  • How digital assets can be bought/sold, transaction fees RPSAD Art. 22
  • List of complementary products (custody, loans, deposits) RPSAD Art. 7(f)
  • Customer support, communication system policy with channels and protocols RPSAD Art. 22 LEAD Art. 21(g)
  • Complaints-handling policy LEAD Art. 21(d)
C. AML/CFT/WMDP Package
  • AML/CFT/WMDP Prevention Policy RPSAD Art. 19
  • Risk Management Procedures RPSAD Arts. 17, 23, 24
  • CDD Policy — standard and enhanced, including PEPs AML Law Art. 15
  • Digital Asset Transaction Limits Policy RPSAD Art. 19
  • Customer/Counterparty Operations Monitoring Policies RPSAD Art. 19
  • Unusual/Suspicious Operation Detection Procedures with flowcharts AML Law Art. 24
  • Regulated Operations Reporting Procedures per UIF requirements UIF Instr. Art. 52
  • Policies to prevent asset theft RPSAD Art. 19
  • Training Plan (annual) AML Law Art. 9(6) UIF Instr. Art. 54
  • Restricted Jurisdictions List (FATF, UN, OFAC) RPSAD Art. 19
D. KYC/KYB/KYT Protocols
  • KYC and KYB procedures — including technical integration with verification provider RPSAD Art. 19
  • KYT (Know Your Transaction) protocol CNAD Guide §2
  • Travel Rule implementation — mandatory for ALL transfers regardless of amount UIF Instr. Art. 84-B(1)(e)
E. Risk Management & Continuity
  • Risk factors description: financial, operational, AML/CFT, cybersecurity RPSAD Art. 21
  • Corporate governance responsibilities within risk framework RPSAD Art. 21
  • Risk management cycle: identification → measurement → monitoring → mitigation → communication RPSAD Art. 21
  • Business Continuity Plan LEAD Art. 21(p)
  • Contingency Plan RPSAD Art. 25(d)(vii)
F. Security & Custody
  • Digital and fiat asset custody policy / segregation of funds and safekeeping LEAD Art. 21(j)
  • Information security and cybersecurity policies: access control, vulnerability management, incident response RPSAD Arts. 25–26
  • ICT security compliance and remediation plan RPSAD Art. 25
  • Geofencing implementation RPSAD Art. 20
G. Operations & Ethics
  • Digital asset operations documentation: spot (Art. 28), term (Art. 29), optional (Art. 30), settlement (Art. 31), seizure (Art. 32), arbitration (Art. 33), buyer protection (Art. 34), investments (Art. 35) RPSAD Arts. 27–35
  • Internal/external audit processes LEAD Art. 21
Digital + physical copies CNAD offices, San Salvador
6

Phase 2 — Definitive Registration

Technical Presentation to Regulator

Detailed technical walkthrough of the platform for CNAD — architecture, security, custody, operations, and KYC/KYB integration.

▾
Presentation Scope
  • Platform architecture and DLT protocols RPSAD Art. 20
  • Computer systems: settlement accuracy, electronic ledger, monitoring capacity RPSAD Art. 20(a)–(d)
  • Security measures and penetration testing results RPSAD Art. 26
  • KYC/KYB technical integration (e.g. SumSub, Jumio) RPSAD Art. 19
  • Custody solutions and asset segregation mechanisms LEAD Art. 21(j)
  • Geofencing and restricted jurisdictions enforcement RPSAD Art. 20
  • ICT vulnerability remediation status RPSAD Art. 25
7

Phase 2 — Definitive Registration

CNAD Review & Resolution

CNAD reviews the full package within 20 business days. May request corrections or additional information.

▾
Review Process
  • CNAD has 20 business days from receipt to issue resolution RPSAD Art. 8 LEAD Art. 20
  • If incomplete: applicant warned, 10 business days to cure (per RPSAD) / 5 business days (per LEAD) RPSAD Art. 8 LEAD Art. 20
  • CNAD may request additional information before the 20-day deadline, specifying compliance term RPSAD Art. 8
  • CNAD considers economic needs of the market and applicant's reputation RPSAD Art. 8
  • Each significant change reported grants CNAD +5 business days RPSAD Art. 9
Possible Outcomes
  • Favorable resolution → proceed to payment RPSAD Art. 8
  • Unfavorable resolution → applicant may resubmit RPSAD Art. 8
8

Phase 2 — Definitive Registration

Corrections & Final Meeting

Address CNAD feedback, submit corrections, and hold a final meeting with the regulator to confirm readiness for operations.

▾
  • Address all CNAD comments and required corrections
  • Each relevant change grants CNAD +5 additional business days for review RPSAD Art. 9
  • Final meeting with regulator to confirm readiness
  • CNAD may verify: Board compliance, CO effectiveness, CDD processes, Travel Rule, PEP processes, beneficial ownership, training plans CNAD Guide §6.b
9

Completion

Payment, Registration & Tax Benefits

Pay registration fee, receive definitive registration number, and activate tax exemptions under Art. 36 LEAD.

▾
  • Pay initial registration fee: $5,475 (15 minimum monthly salaries) within 10 business days of favorable resolution RPSAD Art. 10 LEAD Art. 12(1)(a)
  • Non-payment within 10 days = not registered RPSAD Art. 10
  • Definitive registration number assigned after payment verification RPSAD Art. 11
  • CNAD forwards resolution to Ministry of Finance within 3 business days RPSAD Art. 11
  • General Directorate of Internal Taxes issues tax/duty exemption qualification RPSAD Art. 11
  • CNAD notifies UIF of registration CNAD Procedure
  • Code of Ethics must be prepared within 6 calendar months of registration RPSAD Art. 18
0% Income Tax 0% VAT 0% Capital Gains 0% Municipal Tax

Track 2

Issuer Registration Flow

Separate registration track for entities issuing digital assets. Governed by LEAD Arts. 22–32 and the Regulation of Registration of Issuers and Public and Private Issuances (CNAD Session CNAD-017-2023).

1

Issuer Registration with CNAD

Register the issuing entity before submitting any issuance application. May be done simultaneously with issuance registration.

▾
Required Documents Issuer Reg. Art. 4
  • Sworn statement (notarized affidavit) — information true, accurate, complete Art. 4(a)
  • Articles of incorporation, amendments, bylaws, copy registered with Commerce Registry Art. 4(b)
  • Shareholder list with capital participation, ID, TIN. Copy of ID/TIN for holders of 10%+ of capital. UBO information for corporate shareholders Art. 4(c)
  • Directors list: ID, TIN, Board credentials, term of office, CV showing professional/business experience Art. 4(d)
  • Audited financial statements for last 3 fiscal years by external auditor Art. 4(g)
  • Copy of TIN Art. 4(h)
  • Website URL Art. 4(i)
  • Jurisdictions of operations Art. 4(j)
  • List of digital assets previously issued in public offerings Art. 4(k)

Changes must be reported to CNAD within 10 business days. Issuer and issuance registration may be simultaneous. Art. 4

2

Prepare Relevant Information Document (RID)

Draft the RID per Annex A requirements. Must be fair, clear, and not misleading. Language depends on issuance size.

▾
RID Requirements Issuer Reg. Art. 10
  • If issuance < US$1,000,000 — RID at least in Spanish Art. 10
  • If issuance ≥ US$1,000,000 — RID must be in Spanish AND English Art. 10
RID Content Structure Annex A
  • Summary/Cover: Issuer name, address, activity, description, platforms, amount, trading currency/label
  • Participants: Issuer, intermediaries, certifiers, exchanges, blockchains, auditors, legal advisors
  • Body: Table of contents, cover letter, sworn statement, certifier's report, issuer description (incl. 3-year financial statements per IFRS), public offering characteristics (21 sub-items), fund destination, technology information (DLT, consensus, fees), risk description & management, dispute resolution, tax regime per LEAD Art. 36
Type of Offering must be specified
  • Debt / Equity / Income Annex A §3.f
3

Certifier Evaluation & Report

A registered certifier evaluates the RID and issues a favorable or unfavorable report, sent to CNAD within 5 business days.

▾
  • Issuer engages a registered certifier Issuer Reg. Art. 15(1)
  • Certifier reviews RID + supporting documentation, may request additional info Art. 15(3)
  • Certifier issues favorable/unfavorable report with risk assessment Art. 14
  • Report must contain: detailed risk description (issuer, offering, digital asset, project, technology risks), review of financial projections, reasoned technical opinion Art. 14(d)–(f)
  • Certifier sends report to CNAD within 5 business days Art. 15(6)
  • If no registered certifiers exist, CNAD acts as certifier LEAD Art. 45
4

Issuance Registration with CNAD

Submit complete issuance documentation. CNAD decides within 5 business days.

▾
Issuance Documents Issuer Reg. Art. 5
  • Sworn statement (notarized) Art. 5(a)
  • Board of Directors certification authorizing the issuance Art. 5(b)
  • Certifier's report Art. 5(c)
  • Documents describing fundamental aspects of the issuance Art. 5(d)
  • Listing label/code Art. 5(e)
  • Digital platforms where listed/advertised/traded Art. 5(f)
  • Draft contracts Art. 5(g)
  • All smart contract addresses Art. 5(h)
  • Smart contract audit documentation (if unavailable — disclose in RID risk section) Art. 5(i)
  • Draft RID per Annex A Art. 5(j)
  • Collateral documentation + real estate appraisal if applicable Art. 5(k) Annex D
  • Full identity info of all relevant persons (advisors, dev team, programmers, service providers) Art. 5(l)
CNAD Decision Art. 16
  • CNAD decides within 5 business days of receipt Art. 16
  • If incomplete: max 20 business days to complete Art. 16
  • CNAD considers impact on national financial/monetary stability Art. 16
Post-Authorization
  • CNAD publishes offering announcement within 3 business hours Art. 21
  • Issuer must use qualification within 3 months or it lapses Art. 17(a)
  • Ongoing obligations: continuous information, quarterly reports Art. 23

Issuer Fee: 0.01% of authorized public offering amount + $50 per certification of issuance resolution LEAD Art. 12(2)

🔒

Private Issuance Requirements

  • Addressed to max 50 qualified investors Issuer Reg. Art. 24
  • No mass media or public appeal permitted Art. 24
  • Cannot be sold in public offerings Art. 24
  • No advertising or public communications permitted Art. 28
  • Must keep complete register of acquirers, reported quarterly to CNAD Art. 28
👤

Qualified Investor Definition

  • Min. US$500,000 in investible assets excluding principal domicile Qualified Investors Reg.
  • FSS-authorized institutions, international banks (BCR-registered) Qualified Investors Reg.
  • Autonomous institutions, registered DASPs, public offering issuers Qualified Investors Reg.
  • SPV specifically created for the offering with net worth > $500,000 Qualified Investors Reg.

Documents for Private Issuance Issuer Reg. Art. 25

Entity & Identification

  • Certificate of incorporation + legal rep credentials Art. 25(a)
  • Corporate name, address, legal representative details Art. 25(a)–(b)
  • Principal economic activity Art. 25(c)
  • Type of digital asset to be issued Art. 25(d)

Investor & Financial

  • General investor profile Art. 25(f)
  • Detailed list of potential qualified investors Art. 25(g)
  • Copy of CDD file from DASP Compliance Office Private Issuance Req.
  • 3-year audited financial statements Private Issuance Req.
  • Registration and payment method for acquirers Art. 25(h)

CNAD has 20 business days to communicate non-objection. Positive administrative silence if no response within that term. Issuer Reg. Art. 25

Team Structure

Mandatory Personnel & Residency

Key personnel required for DASP operations with qualification and residency requirements.

👔

Director / Legal Representative

  • Board of Directors or Sole Administrator required RPSAD Art. 7(b)(ii)
  • Credentials registered with Commercial Registry RPSAD Art. 7(b)(ii)
  • Foreign entities: may appoint local representative via POA LEAD Art. 18(d)
No explicit residency requirement

Neither LEAD nor RPSAD require directors to be resident in El Salvador. No nationality requirements exist for any personnel.

🛡️

Principal Compliance Officer

  • Appointed by Board of Directors AML Law Art. 20
  • Must hold AML/CFT/WMDP prevention certification UIF Instr. Art. 64
  • Registered with UIF within 5 business days of appointment UIF Instr. Art. 64
  • Training in AML/CFT prevention + regulatory framework knowledge UIF Instr. Art. 72
  • Sufficient experience in the entity's economic activity UIF Instr. Art. 72(2)
  • Independence and autonomy from operational areas UIF Instr. Art. 84-B(2)
  • Highest hierarchical rank among direct reports to CEO/GM AML Law Art. 20
No residency requirement for DASPs

Explicit exemption: DASPs' COs are NOT required to reside in the country UIF Instr. Art. 84-B(2). However, must maintain employment relationship ("dependencia laboral") with the entity. Other supervised entities' COs MUST be resident UIF Instr. Arts. 64, 72, 80.

🛡️

Substitute Compliance Officer

  • Mandatory by law — not optional AML Law Art. 20
  • Same qualifications as Principal CO AML Law Art. 20
  • Must hold AML/CFT certification UIF Instr. Art. 64
  • Registered with UIF UIF Instr. Art. 3
  • Acts in absence of Principal CO
No residency requirement for DASPs

Same exemption as Principal CO per UIF Instr. Art. 84-B(2).

💻

CISO / Information Security Officer

  • May be a Board member or manager RPSAD Art. 25(a)
  • Coordinates the cybersecurity plan RPSAD Art. 25(a)
  • Must have authority and independence in management RPSAD Art. 25(a)
  • Direct access to Board of Directors or President RPSAD Art. 25(a)
  • Oversees incident response, vulnerability management, security testing RPSAD Arts. 25–26
No residency requirement

No residency requirement stated in RPSAD. Must have direct access to Board.

Prevention Committee AML Law Art. 23

DASPs that designate a CO must also constitute a Prevention Committee with at least 3 members: one from the Board of Directors, the Compliance Officer, and additional member(s). Natural person DASPs may opt not to designate a CO — in that case, they personally assume all AML responsibilities. AML Law Art. 22

  • Local bank account required

  • Records maintained for minimum 15 years AML Law Art. 26

Dual Supervision Structure

SSF (Superintendencia del Sistema Financiero) supervises DASPs operating with Bitcoin. CNAD supervises DASPs regarding all other digital assets. Entities supervised by SSF may also register as DASP if they meet all requirements. AML Law Art. 12 RPSAD Art. 46

Anti-Money Laundering

AML & Compliance Framework

Obligations under AML Law 2025 (Decreto No. 426), UIF Instructivo, FATF standards, and CNAD Guide for DASPs.

🔍

Customer Due Diligence

  • $1,000 threshold for occasional transactions triggering CDD for DASPs CNAD Guide §2
  • 3 levels: simplified, standard, enhanced AML Law Art. 15
  • Standard CDD at relationship initiation UIF Instr. Art. 14-A(I)
  • Enhanced CDD for high-risk clients: source of wealth/funds, senior management approval UIF Instr. Art. 13
  • PEP (Politically Exposed Persons) policy required RPSAD Art. 19
👥

Beneficial Ownership

  • Identify natural persons owning/controlling ≥ 25% of capital/voting rights AML Law Art. 15
  • UIF Instructivo sets stricter threshold: ≥ 10% UIF Instr. Art. 20(b)
  • "Special care and diligence" for DASPs in applying UBO requirements CNAD Guide §3
  • Particularly relevant in transfers/exchanges of digital assets CNAD Guide §3
  • For trusts: identify settlors, controllers, beneficiaries UIF Instr. Art. 21
✈️

Travel Rule

  • Mandatory for ALL transfers regardless of amount UIF Instr. Art. 84-B(1)(e) CNAD Guide §2
  • Collect: date, type/quantity, names/addresses, DOB, account numbers CNAD Guide §2
  • Transaction identifiers: sending/receiving addresses, exchange rates CNAD Guide §2
  • "Under no circumstances should DASPs fail to collect Travel Rule information" CNAD Guide §2
🚨

Suspicious Operations

  • Report to UIF within 24 hours after completing analysis AML Law Art. 24
  • Max 15 business days for analysis from detection (extendable once) AML Law Art. 24
  • Must report attempted suspicious transactions AML Law Art. 24
  • Transaction amount is irrelevant for suspicious reporting AML Law Art. 24
  • Tipping off prohibition — CO must not alert the client UIF Instr. Art. 46
  • SOR is confidential, not a criminal complaint AML Law Art. 28 UIF Instr. Art. 44
📊

Regulated Operations Reporting

  • Cash transactions > $10,000 (individual) or accumulated per month UIF Instr. Art. 51
  • Other means > $25,000 (individual) or accumulated per month UIF Instr. Art. 51
  • International electronic transfers ≥ $1,000 UIF Instr. Art. 52
  • Reporting deadline: 5 business days UIF Instr. Art. 51
  • DASPs use UIF-issued reporting forms UIF Instr. Art. 84-C
📚

Training & Records

  • Annual training plan by CO, approved by Board by Dec 31 each year UIF Instr. Art. 54
  • All employees trained at least once/year UIF Instr. Art. 54
  • Mandatory AML induction for all new employees UIF Instr. Art. 55
  • All records retained minimum 15 years AML Law Art. 26 UIF Instr. Art. 57
  • Must allow reconstruction of each transaction AML Law Art. 26

Information Security

Cybersecurity Requirements

RPSAD Arts. 25–26 mandate comprehensive cybersecurity governance, real-time monitoring, and periodic testing.

Governance Art. 25(a)

Internal cyber risk procedures. Internal assessments + external audits by independent parties. CISO appointed with authority, independence, and direct Board access.

Identification Art. 25(b)

Safeguard mechanisms. Identify weaknesses. Protect: order management, custody/supervision, information dissemination, non-public acquirer info systems.

Protection Art. 25(c)

Security operations center. Antivirus & intrusion prevention. Acquirer info protection. Internal threat detection. Employee access controls. Cybersecurity training for all employees.

Detection Art. 25(d)

Continuous real-time monitoring. External/internal factor monitoring. Multi-level security and access controls.

Response & Recovery Art. 25(d)(vi–viii)

Resume critical processes within 6 hours. Settle unfinished transactions within 24 hours. Contingency plan for orderly cessation if 6-hour recovery impossible. Regularly tested plans.

Security Testing Art. 26

Periodic rigorous testing by external cybersecurity auditor. 3 mandatory scenarios: confidentiality breach, availability breach, integrity breakdown. Each with vulnerability assessment + intrusion testing.

Incentives

Tax Benefits

Registered DASPs, issuers, and acquirers enjoy comprehensive tax exemptions under LEAD Art. 36.

0% Income Tax

All yields and income from digital assets exempt from income tax. LEAD Art. 36(b)(c)

0% Capital Gains

Capital gains from purchase, sale, or any transfer of digital assets exempt from all taxation. LEAD Art. 36(b)

0% VAT (IVA)

Exempt from Transfer of Movable Goods tax. No withholding obligations. LEAD Art. 36(c)

0% Municipal Tax

Exempt from municipal taxes and any other taxation related to digital asset activities. LEAD Art. 36(c)

Shareholder Pass-Through

Tax benefits apply individually to partners/shareholders for profits and dividends from digital asset activities. LEAD Art. 36(d)

Activation

Benefits apply from date of provisional registration. CNAD notifies Ministry of Finance within 3 business days. RPSAD Art. 11

Exception LEAD Art. 36(e)

Benefits do NOT apply to exchange of digital assets for goods/services not detailed in Art. 19 of LEAD. Non-payment of annual renewal fee = automatic loss of all tax benefits. RPSAD Art. 12
