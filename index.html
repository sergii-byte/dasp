Regulatory Stack

The binding sources, in one view

Both guides cite only currently-binding statutes, regulations and supervisory rules. Outdated references (including pre-reform CNAD Guide §2 citations) have been removed across the portal.

Statute · DASP regime
LEAD

Ley de Emisión de Activos Digitales — Decreto No. 643 of 9 January 2023. Establishes the DASP / PSAD legal status, perimeter of regulated activities, CNAD as competent authority, tax exemption regime.

Licensing
Regulation · DASP
RPSAD

Reglamento de Proveedores de Servicios de Activos Digitales — CNAD Resolución. Operational rulebook for PSAD: registration mechanics, capital, governance, cybersecurity, ongoing reporting to CNAD.

Licensing + Ops
Statute · AML/CTF
AML Law (D-426)

Decree No. 426 — Special Law for the Prevention, Control and Punishment of Asset Laundering, Financing of Terrorism, and Financing of the Proliferation of Weapons of Mass Destruction. Defines reporting entities (Art. 7) including DASPs (via supervision by CNAD), CDD trigger (Art. 15), 24-hour STR clock (Art. 24), regulated transactions reporting incl. digital assets (Art. 25), 15-year retention (Art. 26), tipping-off prohibition (Art. 44).

Compliance
Operational rules · UIF
Instructivo UIF

Operational AML rulebook from the Financial Investigation Unit (Reform 1 + Reform 2). Standard CDD with identity verification (Art. 12), EDD (Art. 13), SDD (Art. 14), PEP measures (Arts. 16-18), beneficial owner 10% floor (Art. 20) + UBO majority/control (Art. 21-A), Operación Regulada thresholds (Art. 51), Travel Rule data (Art. 52 par. 2 reformed), VASP-specific obligations (Arts. 84-A, 84-B, 84-C — Reform 1).

Compliance
Statute · Counter-terrorism
LECAT

D-108 — Special Law against Acts of Terrorism. UNSC sanctions freeze obligation (Art. 37), terrorist designation before UNSC (Art. 34-B). Tipping-off is in D-426 Art. 44 (not LECAT).
