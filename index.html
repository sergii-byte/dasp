Track 1

DASP Registration Flow

Two-phase process administered by CNAD under LEAD Arts. 18–21 and RPSAD Arts. 5–14. Estimated total duration: 8–12 months from entity formation to operational license.

Entity 2–4 wk
Personnel 2–4 wk
Pre-Reg 2–4 wk
Docs & Policies 8–16 wk
CNAD Review 4–8 wk
Final 2–4 wk

Document preparation (AML policies, business plan, cybersecurity framework) is the longest phase and overlaps with earlier stages. Practical experience shows 8–12 months end-to-end including multiple rounds of CNAD feedback and corrections.

1

Phase 1 — Pre-Registration

Entity Formation & Incorporation

Incorporate a Salvadoran S.A. de C.V. or register a branch of a foreign company. Minimum share capital $2,000 (5% paid at incorporation).

▾
Legal Requirements
  • Natural persons: present DUI or resident card, indicate domicile in El Salvador LEAD Art. 18(c)
  • Foreign legal entities: must form a corporation or branch domiciled in El Salvador, registered with the National Registry Center (CNR) LEAD Art. 18(d)
  • Obtain Tax Identification Number (NIT) RPSAD Art. 7(b)(iii)
  • Register Board of Directors / Sole Administrator credentials with Commercial Registry RPSAD Art. 7(b)(ii)
  • Minimum share capital: $2,000 (5% = $100 paid at incorporation)
Documents
  • Notarized public deed of incorporation, registered with Registry of Commerce RPSAD Art. 7(b)(i)
  • Credential of Board of Directors election or general power of attorney RPSAD Art. 7(b)(ii)
  • Copy of Tax Identification Number card RPSAD Art. 7(b)(iii)
  • Company registration certificate RPSAD Art. 7(d)
Duration: 2–4 weeks
2

Phase 1 — Pre-Registration

Mandatory Personnel Onboarding

Appoint Principal and Substitute Compliance Officers and designate a CISO. Register COs with UIF before application.

▾
Required Appointments
  • Principal Compliance Officer — appointed by Board of Directors, AML/CFT/WMDP certified LEAD Art. 21 UIF Instr. Art. 84-B(2)
  • Substitute Compliance Officer — mandatory by law, same qualifications AML Law Art. 20
  • CISO / Information Security Officer — Board member or manager with authority and direct Board access RPSAD Art. 25(a)
CO Qualifications
  • AML/CFT/FPADM prevention training UIF Instr. Art. 72
  • Knowledge of regulatory framework UIF Instr. Art. 72
  • Sufficient experience in the entity's economic activity UIF Instr. Art. 72(2)
  • No residency requirement for DASP COs — explicit exemption UIF Instr. Art. 84-B(2)
  • Must maintain employment relationship with the entity UIF Instr. Art. 84-B(2)
Documents
  • Board resolution appointing Principal and Substitute CO AML Law Art. 20
  • AML/CFT certifications for both COs UIF Instr. Art. 64
  • UIF platform registration for both COs UIF Instr. Art. 3
  • Organization chart showing CO independence from operations RPSAD Art. 7(e)
Duration: 2–4 weeks UIF registration: 5 days from appointment UIF Instr. Art. 64 CO residency exemption for DASPs
3

Phase 1 — Pre-Registration

Pre-Registration Application & First Presentation

Submit pre-registration form to CNAD with applicant details and desired operating area. Present the product/service — CNAD issues "Objection" or "No Objection".

▾
Process
  • Complete CNAD pre-registration form (electronic) CNAD Procedure
  • Provide: applicant details, desired operating area, description of services LEAD Art. 18
  • CNAD reviews territorial/material competence per LEAD Arts. 2–4 CNAD Procedure
  • Present business model and digital asset services per LEAD Art. 19 categories
  • Provide list of digital assets with benefits, restrictions, limits RPSAD Art. 7(h)
  • Documentation demonstrating capacity to offer indicated services RPSAD Art. 7(g)
Outcome
  • "No Objection" → CNAD specifies registration requirements → proceed to Phase 2 CNAD Procedure
  • "Objection" → address concerns based on territorial/material competence → may resubmit CNAD Procedure
Duration: 2–4 weeks
4

Phase 2 — Definitive Registration

Full Documentation Package

Prepare and submit the complete document package per LEAD Art. 20 and RPSAD Arts. 7, 17–35. This is the most time-intensive phase — typically 2–4 months of preparation.

▾
A. Business & Corporate
  • 3-Year Business Plan for El Salvador: executive summary, market strategy, financial projections, KPIs, risks & mitigations CNAD Procedure §1
  • Detailed organizational structure — names, positions, functions, international structure RPSAD Art. 7(e) LEAD Art. 20(c)
  • Corporate governance document RPSAD Art. 7(e)
B. Customer Service & Communication
  • Description of each digital asset: characteristics, price, 12-month history, volume RPSAD Art. 22
  • How assets can be bought/sold, transaction fees RPSAD Art. 22
  • Complementary products (custody, loans, deposits) RPSAD Art. 7(f)
  • Customer support & communication system policy RPSAD Art. 22 LEAD Art. 21(g)
  • Complaints-handling policy LEAD Art. 21(d)
C. AML/CFT/WMDP Package
  • AML/CFT/WMDP Prevention Policy RPSAD Art. 19
  • Risk Management Procedures RPSAD Arts. 17, 23, 24
  • CDD Policy — standard and enhanced, including PEPs AML Law Art. 15
  • Transaction Limits Policy RPSAD Art. 19
  • Customer/Counterparty Monitoring Policies RPSAD Art. 19
  • Suspicious Operation Detection Procedures with flowcharts AML Law Art. 24
  • Regulated Operations Reporting Procedures UIF Instr. Art. 52
  • Asset theft prevention policies RPSAD Art. 19
  • Annual Training Plan AML Law Art. 9(6) UIF Instr. Art. 54
  • Restricted Jurisdictions List RPSAD Art. 19
D. KYC/KYB/KYT
  • KYC and KYB procedures with technical integration documentation RPSAD Art. 19
  • KYT (Know Your Transaction) protocol CNAD Guide §2
  • Travel Rule implementation — mandatory for ALL transfers regardless of amount UIF Instr. Art. 84-B(1)(e)
E. Risk Management & Continuity
  • Risk factors: financial, operational, AML/CFT, cybersecurity RPSAD Art. 21
  • Risk management cycle: identification → measurement → monitoring → mitigation → communication RPSAD Art. 21
  • Business Continuity Plan LEAD Art. 21(p)
  • Contingency Plan RPSAD Art. 25(d)(vii)
F. Security & Custody
  • Digital and fiat asset custody policy, segregation of funds LEAD Art. 21(j)
  • Cybersecurity policies: access control, vulnerability management, incident response RPSAD Arts. 25–26
  • ICT security compliance and remediation plan RPSAD Art. 25
  • Geofencing implementation RPSAD Art. 20
G. Operations
  • Digital asset operations: spot Art. 28, term Art. 29, optional Art. 30, settlement Art. 31, seizure Art. 32, arbitration Art. 33, buyer protection Art. 34, investments Art. 35
  • Internal/external audit framework LEAD Art. 21
Duration: 8–16 weeks (preparation) Digital + physical copies to CNAD

Phase 2 — Definitive Registration

Technical Presentation to Regulator

Detailed technical walkthrough of the platform for CNAD — architecture, security, custody, operations, and KYC/KYB integration.

▾
  • Platform architecture and DLT protocols RPSAD Art. 20
  • Computer systems: settlement accuracy, electronic ledger, monitoring capacity RPSAD Art. 20(a)–(d)
  • Security measures and penetration testing results RPSAD Art. 26
  • KYC/KYB technical integration RPSAD Art. 19
  • Custody solutions and asset segregation LEAD Art. 21(j)
  • Geofencing and restricted jurisdictions enforcement RPSAD Art. 20
6

Phase 2 — Definitive Registration

CNAD Review, Corrections & Final Meeting

CNAD reviews the full package within 20 business days. Expect multiple rounds of feedback, corrections, and supplementary submissions.

▾
Review Process
  • CNAD has 20 business days from receipt to issue resolution RPSAD Art. 8 LEAD Art. 20
  • If incomplete: 10 business days to cure RPSAD Art. 8
  • CNAD may request additional information before the 20-day deadline, specifying compliance term RPSAD Art. 8
  • CNAD considers economic needs of the market and applicant's reputation RPSAD Art. 8
  • Each significant change reported grants CNAD +5 business days RPSAD Art. 9
Corrections & Final Meeting
  • Address all CNAD comments and required corrections (expect 1–3 rounds)
  • Submit supplementary documentation (security remediation plans, updated policies)
  • Final meeting with regulator — CNAD verifies: Board compliance, CO effectiveness, CDD processes, Travel Rule, PEP processes, beneficial ownership, training plans CNAD Guide §6.b
Outcome
  • Favorable resolution → proceed to payment RPSAD Art. 8
  • Unfavorable resolution → may resubmit RPSAD Art. 8
CNAD review: 20 bus. days RPSAD Art. 8 Cure period: 10 bus. days RPSAD Art. 8 Per change: +5 bus. days RPSAD Art. 9
7

Completion

Payment, Registration & Tax Benefits Activation

Pay registration fee, receive definitive registration number, and activate full tax exemptions under LEAD Art. 36.

▾
  • Pay registration fee: $5,475 (15 min. salaries) within 10 business days of favorable resolution RPSAD Art. 10 LEAD Art. 12(1)(a)
  • Non-payment = not registered RPSAD Art. 10
  • Definitive registration number assigned RPSAD Art. 11
  • CNAD forwards resolution to Ministry of Finance within 3 business days RPSAD Art. 11
  • Tax exemption qualification issued RPSAD Art. 11
  • CNAD notifies UIF of registration CNAD Procedure
  • Code of Ethics: prepare within 6 calendar months of registration RPSAD Art. 18
Payment: 10 bus. days RPSAD Art. 10 MoF notification: 3 bus. days RPSAD Art. 11 Code of Ethics: 6 months RPSAD Art. 18 0% Income Tax 0% VAT 0% Capital Gains

Post-Registration Obligations

Annual renewal fee: $3,650 due Q1 each year RPSAD Art. 12. Quarterly reports to CNAD within first 20 days of Apr/Jul/Oct/Jan RPSAD Arts. 15–16. Non-payment = registration cancelled + loss of tax benefits RPSAD Art. 12. Records retained 15 years minimum AML Law Art. 26.

Track 2

Issuer Registration Flow

Separate registration for entities issuing digital assets. Governed by LEAD Arts. 22–32 and the Regulation of Registration of Issuers and Public and Private Issuances (CNAD-017-2023).

1

Issuer Registration with CNAD

Register the issuing entity. May be done simultaneously with issuance registration.

▾
Documents Issuer Reg. Art. 4
  • Sworn statement (notarized) — information true, accurate, complete Art. 4(a)
  • Articles of incorporation, amendments, bylaws Art. 4(b)
  • Shareholder list: capital participation, ID, TIN. 10%+ holders: ID/TIN copies. UBO for corporate shareholders Art. 4(c)
  • Directors list: ID, TIN, credentials, term, CV with professional experience Art. 4(d)
  • Audited financial statements — last 3 fiscal years Art. 4(g)
  • TIN copy, website URL, jurisdictions of operations, list of previous issuances Art. 4(h)–(k)
Changes: report within 10 bus. days Art. 4
2

Prepare RID (Relevant Information Document)

Draft the RID per Annex A — must be fair, clear, not misleading. Language depends on issuance size.

▾
  • Issuance < $1M → RID in Spanish only Issuer Reg. Art. 10
  • Issuance ≥ $1M → RID in Spanish + English Art. 10
RID Structure Annex A
  • Summary/Cover: issuer name, address, activity, platforms, amount, trading label
  • Participants: issuer, intermediaries, exchanges, blockchains, auditors, legal advisors
  • Body: sworn statement, issuer description (3-year IFRS financials), offering characteristics (21 sub-items incl. type: Debt/Equity/Income), fund destination, technology info (DLT, consensus, fees), risk description & management, dispute resolution, tax regime
3

Certifier Evaluation → CNAD Decision

Registered certifier evaluates the RID, sends report to CNAD. CNAD decides within 5 business days.

▾
  • Certifier reviews RID + supporting docs, issues favorable/unfavorable report Issuer Reg. Art. 14
  • Report: risk assessment (issuer, offering, digital asset, project, technology), financial projection review, reasoned technical opinion Art. 14(d)–(f)
  • Certifier sends report to CNAD within 5 business days Art. 15(6)
  • CNAD decides within 5 business days of receipt Art. 16
  • If incomplete: max 20 business days to complete Art. 16
  • Issuer must use qualification within 3 months or it lapses Art. 17(a)
  • If no registered certifiers: CNAD acts as certifier LEAD Art. 45
Issuance Documents Issuer Reg. Art. 5
  • Sworn statement + Board certification authorizing issuance Art. 5(a)–(b)
  • Certifier's report Art. 5(c)
  • Digital platforms list, draft contracts, smart contract addresses + audit Art. 5(f)–(i)
  • Draft RID per Annex A Art. 5(j)
  • Collateral documentation if applicable Art. 5(k) Annex D
  • Full identity info of all relevant persons (advisors, dev team, service providers) Art. 5(l)
CNAD decision: 5 bus. days Art. 16 Use within: 3 months Art. 17(a)

Fee: 0.01% of authorized public offering amount + $50 per certification LEAD Art. 12(2)

CNAD has 20 business days to communicate non-objection. Positive administrative silence if no response.

Team Structure

Mandatory Personnel & Residency

Key personnel required for DASP operations with qualification and residency requirements.

👔

Director / Legal Representative

  • Board of Directors or Sole Administrator RPSAD Art. 7(b)(ii)
  • Credentials registered with Commercial Registry
  • Foreign entities: local representative via POA LEAD Art. 18(d)
No explicit residency requirement

Neither LEAD nor RPSAD require directors to be resident. No nationality requirements.

🛡️

Principal Compliance Officer

  • Appointed by Board AML Law Art. 20
  • AML/CFT certification UIF Instr. Art. 64
  • UIF registration within 5 days UIF Instr. Art. 64
  • Independence from operational areas UIF Instr. Art. 84-B(2)
  • Highest rank among direct reports to CEO AML Law Art. 20
No residency for DASPs

Explicit exemption: DASP COs not required to reside in El Salvador UIF Instr. Art. 84-B(2). Must have employment relationship. Other entities' COs must be resident Arts. 64, 72, 80.

🛡️

Substitute Compliance Officer

  • Mandatory — not optional AML Law Art. 20
  • Same qualifications as Principal CO
  • AML/CFT certification + UIF registration UIF Instr. Arts. 3, 64
  • Acts in absence of Principal CO
No residency for DASPs

Same exemption as Principal CO UIF Instr. Art. 84-B(2).

💻

CISO / Information Security Officer

  • Board member or manager RPSAD Art. 25(a)
  • Coordinates cybersecurity plan
  • Authority and independence in management RPSAD Art. 25(a)
  • Direct access to Board or President RPSAD Art. 25(a)
No residency requirement

Prevention Committee AML Law Art. 23

DASPs must constitute a Prevention Committee: at least 3 members including one Board member and the CO. Natural person DASPs may opt not to designate a CO — they personally assume all AML responsibilities AML Law Art. 22.

Information Security

Cybersecurity Requirements

RPSAD Arts. 25–26: comprehensive governance, real-time monitoring, periodic testing.

Governance Art. 25(a)

Internal cyber risk procedures. Internal assessments + external audits. CISO with authority, independence, direct Board access.

Identification Art. 25(b)

Safeguard mechanisms. Identify weaknesses. Protect: order management, custody, info dissemination, acquirer data.

Protection Art. 25(c)

SOC, antivirus, intrusion prevention. Acquirer info protection. Internal threat detection. Employee access controls. Training.

Detection Art. 25(d)

Continuous real-time monitoring. External/internal risk factors. Multi-level security and access controls.

Response & Recovery Art. 25(d)(vi)

Resume critical processes: 6 hours. Settle transactions: 24 hours. Contingency plan for orderly cessation if unrecoverable.

Testing Art. 26

External cybersecurity auditor. 3 scenarios: confidentiality, availability, integrity breach. Vulnerability assessment + intrusion testing.

Incentives

Tax Benefits LEAD Art. 36

Registered DASPs, issuers, and acquirers enjoy comprehensive tax exemptions on digital asset activities.

0% Income Tax

All yields and income from digital assets exempt. Art. 36(b)(c)

0% Capital Gains

Purchase, sale, or any transfer exempt from all taxation. Art. 36(b)

0% VAT (IVA)

Exempt from IVA. No withholding obligations. Art. 36(c)

0% Municipal Tax

Exempt from all municipal taxes on digital asset activities. Art. 36(c)

Shareholder Pass-Through

Benefits apply to partners/shareholders individually. Art. 36(d)

Activation

From provisional registration date. CNAD notifies MoF within 3 days. RPSAD Art. 11

Exception LEAD Art. 36(e)

Benefits do NOT apply to exchange of digital assets for goods/services outside Art. 19 of LEAD. Non-payment of annual renewal = automatic loss of all tax benefits RPSAD Art. 12.
