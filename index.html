Regulatory Stack

The binding sources, in one view

Two parallel verticals govern a DASP — the LEAD vertical (DASP-licensing rails) and the AML/CTF vertical (financial-crime prevention). Each vertical has a statute → regulation → operational guide ladder. Where rules collide, hierarchy controls: statute > regulation > guide; within the AML stack, D-426/2025 Art. 59 (Specialty) makes the new statute prevail over any contradicting rule, and Art. 61 transitional keeps the prior Reglamento, Instructivo and Acuerdo 734 alive only insofar as they do not contradict the new law.

LEAD
D-643/2023 · statute
in force 17 Jan 2023
Ley de Emisión de Activos Digitales
Digital Asset Issuance Law

Establishes the digital-asset legal regime, creates CNAD as the sectoral supervisor, defines the regulated DASP activities and the tax framework.

Licensing
RPSAD
CNAD-035-2023 · regulation
in force 2023
Reglamento de los Proveedores de Servicios de Activos Digitales
Regulation for Digital Asset Service Providers

Implements LEAD. Registration procedure, prudential and conduct rules, governance, cybersecurity, ongoing CNAD reporting.

DASP Registration Application Guide

CNAD's procedural guide for the PSAD application — documentation requirements, risk-management coverage, platform validation.

LCLDA
D-426/2025 · statute
in force 17 Oct 2025
Ley Especial para la Prevención, Control y Sanción del Lavado de Activos, Financiamiento del Terrorismo y Financiamiento de la Proliferación de Armas de Destrucción Masiva
Special Law for the Prevention, Control and Sanctioning of Asset Laundering, Financing of Terrorism and Financing of the Proliferation of Weapons of Mass Destruction

The current AML/CTF/CFP statute. Replaces D-498/1998. Prevails over conflicting rules by Art. 59 Specialty.

LECAT
D-108/2006 · statute
reformed by D-928/2024
in force from 9 Jan 2024
Ley Especial Contra Actos de Terrorismo
Special Law Against Acts of Terrorism

Counter-terrorism offences and the UNSC sanctions-freeze obligation binding every Reporting Entity. Tipping-off prohibition is in LCLDA, not here.

UIF Instructive for the Prevention, Detection and Control of Money Laundering, Asset Laundering, Terrorism Financing and Financing of Proliferation of Weapons of Mass Destruction

UIF's operational AML rulebook for all Reporting Entities — CDD / EDD / SDD, PEP, beneficial ownership, reporting mechanics. Transitional under D-426 Art. 61.
